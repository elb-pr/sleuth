Task 4.1, **Enquiry Recording and Assignment**, establishes the real-time operational audit trail for every investigative action. This task is executed within the **Task Log / Actions Register**, which serves as the contemporaneous record of who did what, when, and under whose authority. Without this log, the investigation cannot demonstrate that its collection activities were authorised, proportionate, and systematically conducted.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Mandatory Fields for Every Logged Action
Each entry in the Task Log must capture a minimum set of structured fields to ensure traceability and accountability:
*   **Unique Reference Number:** A sequential identifier (e.g., ACT-001, ACT-002) that links the action to the broader case file and enables cross-referencing with the **Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP)** and the **Case Decision Log (CDL)**.
*   **Date and Time of Entry:** A UTC-normalised timestamp recording when the entry was made. In professional or legal settings, this must be contemporaneous — logged at the time the action occurs, not retrospectively.
*   **Assigned Investigator:** The identity of the individual responsible for executing the action. This satisfies the "Accountability" requirement of the **PLAN** framework established in Phase 1 (Task 1.3).
*   **Task Description:** A specific, actionable description of the enquiry to be conducted. Vague entries (e.g., "look into Entity A") are prohibited; the description must define the exact source to be queried, the specific information sought, and the expected output format.
*   **Linked Intelligence Requirement (IR):** Every action must reference the specific IR from the ICP that it is designed to address. Actions that cannot be linked to a documented IR indicate potential scope creep and must be justified through a policy deviation entry (Task 4.3).
*   **Deadline:** A target completion date to ensure the investigation maintains operational tempo and that overdue actions are flagged for supervisory review.
*   **Status:** A tracked field recording whether the action is Pending, In Progress, Complete, or Cancelled. Status changes must be timestamped.

### 2. Contemporaneous Recording Standards
The legal and methodological value of the Task Log depends entirely on its contemporaneity — entries made days or weeks after the fact are vulnerable to challenge.
*   **Real-Time Entry:** Actions must be logged as they are initiated, not upon completion. This captures the investigator's "true state of mind" at the time of the decision, preventing hindsight bias.
*   **Digital Timestamping:** Where digital case management systems are used (e.g., Kaseware, Hunchly), entries are automatically timestamped and digitally signed. Where electronic access is unavailable, standard procedure requires sequentially numbered, bound paper books with blank spaces struck through to prevent retroactive alteration.
*   **No Retroactive Editing:** Once an entry is logged, it may be amended or supplemented with additional notes, but the original entry must remain visible and unaltered. Amendments are recorded as new entries referencing the original action number.

### 3. Assignment and Delegation Protocols
In multi-investigator operations, the Task Log governs the distribution of work.
*   **Tasking Authority:** Only authorised individuals (typically the lead investigator or case supervisor) may create and assign new actions. This prevents unsanctioned collection activities that could breach the **Terms of Reference (ToR)**.
*   **Acknowledgement of Receipt:** The assigned investigator must confirm receipt of the tasking, creating a two-way accountability record.
*   **Escalation Triggers:** If an action reveals information that significantly alters the scope or direction of the investigation, the assigned investigator must escalate to the tasking authority rather than independently expanding the enquiry. This escalation is itself logged as a new action.

### 4. Integration with the Intelligence Collection Plan
The Task Log is not an independent document — it is the operational execution layer of the ICP defined in Phase 1 (Task 2.2).
*   **Gap Tracking:** As actions are completed and their outputs recorded, the ICP is updated to reflect which knowledge gaps have been addressed and which remain open.
*   **Priority Rebalancing:** Completed actions may reveal new leads or render existing IRs obsolete. The Task Log provides the evidential basis for the lead investigator to rebalance priorities within the ICP under supervisory oversight.
*   **Supervisory Review Checkpoint:** Before any action is marked as "Complete", the outcome must be reviewed by a supervisor to verify the quality and relevance of the findings. This checkpoint is mandated by the Administrative Checkpoints for Phase 2.
