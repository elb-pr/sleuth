Phase 2 of the investigative workflow, **Intelligence Collection & Epistemic Filtering**, represents the transition from strategic planning to active field and digital operations. This phase is governed by the "Evaluation" stage of the Intelligence Cycle, ensuring that the raw data entering the system is filtered through a rigorous epistemic gate to prevent "workspace poisoning" by unverified or deceptive information.

### Step 3: Initial Vetting and Epistemic Filtering
The primary tool for this step is the **Source & Credibility Grader Template**. As raw data is collected, it must be independently assessed for the reliability of its origin and the logical credibility of the claim itself.

**Task 3.1: Application of the Admiralty 6x6 Matrix**
Every discrete piece of information must be assigned a two-part alphanumeric grade based on the NATO Intelligence Grading System:
*   **Source Reliability (A–F):** An assessment of the provider’s historical track record, trustworthiness, and competency.
    *   *A (Completely Reliable):* No doubt about authenticity or history.
    *   *F (Cannot Be Judged):* Default for new sources with no established track record.
*   **Information Credibility (1–6):** An assessment of the data’s internal logic and external corroboration.
    *   *1 (Confirmed):* Corroborated by other independent sources.
    *   *6 (Truth Cannot Be Judged):* Insufficient basis for evaluation.

**Task 3.2: Implementation of the "Parallel Sourcing" Mandate**
To mitigate single-source reliance, investigators must seek corroboration across a tiered hierarchy:
*   **Tier 1:** Official government records (e.g., electoral registers, land registries).
*   **Tier 2:** Verified commercial databases (e.g., Companies House, credit reference data).
*   **Tier 3:** Authenticated social media and self-published content.
*   **Tier 4:** Secondary commentary and news aggregation.

**Task 3.3: OSINT Technical Verification**
For digital media, investigators must execute technical vetting subtasks using the **Content Verification Report**:
*   **Reverse Image/Video Search:** Identify original context and previous appearances of the media.
*   **Geolocation:** Pinpointing precise locations through visual cues and satellite imagery comparison.
*   **Chronolocation:** Determining time of capture through shadow analysis (SunCalc), weather records, and vegetation growth.

### Step 4: Continuous Logging and Operational Accountability
As enquiries are executed, they must be recorded in real-time within the **Task Log / Actions Register**. This ensures the investigation remains auditable and that no "scope creep" occurs.

**Task 4.1: Enquiry Recording and Assignment**
Every investigative action is logged with a unique reference number, the identity of the assigned investigator, the specific task description, and the deadline.

**Task 4.2: Mandatory Recording of "Negative Results"**
A critical subtask is the explicit documentation of failed enquiries (e.g., searching a database and finding nothing). This proves that "reasonable lines of enquiry" were pursued, which is essential for legal disclosure and avoiding accusations of narrative bias.

**Task 4.3: Real-Time Policy Deviation Logging**
If the investigator deviates from the original **Investigation Plan**, the rationale must be entered into the log immediately. These entries must be contemporaneous to reflect the investigator's "true state of mind" at the time, preventing hindsight bias.

### Step 5: Evidence Preservation and Digital Chain of Custody
The **Evidence / Material Register** is used to manage all captured digital and physical assets, ensuring they are preserved in a manner suitable for legal or regulatory scrutiny.

**Task 5.1: Digital Evidence Hashing**
Every digital file or screenshot collected must be processed through a cryptographic hashing algorithm (e.g., SHA-256). This "digital fingerprint" ensures that the material cannot be altered without detection, maintaining its integrity for the life of the investigation.

**Task 5.2: Metadata Preservation and Archiving**
Investigators must ensure that all collected imagery retains its EXIF metadata (date, time, and camera settings). Digital pages should be archived using specialized tools (e.g., Hunchly or Archive.today) to capture the URL, timestamp, and IP footprints left behind during the collection session.

**Task 5.3: Maintaining the Source Register**
Parallel to the evidence itself, investigators maintain a **Source Register** that catalogs every source used, its reliability rating (from Step 3), and any specific handling restrictions or access methods required.

### Administrative Checkpoints for Phase 2
*   **Supervisory Review of Task Outcomes:** Managers must review task outcomes before they are marked as "Complete" in the Actions Register to ensure the quality of findings.
*   **Update to the Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP):** As gaps are filled or new leads emerge, the ICP from Phase 1 is updated to reflect new priorities.
*   **Chain of Custody Audit:** Periodic verification that all items in the Evidence Register have valid hash values and secure storage locations.
