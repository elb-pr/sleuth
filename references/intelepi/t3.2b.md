Task 3.2b, **Implementation of the "Parallel Sourcing" Mandate**, is the procedural safeguard against single-source reliance — the investigative equivalent of building a case on one witness. This task operationalises the **Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP)** established in Phase 1 (Task 2.2) by mandating that every significant claim be corroborated across independent source tiers before it can be treated as verified intelligence.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. The Tiered Source Hierarchy
The parallel sourcing strategy organises all available sources into a ranked hierarchy based on their inherent evidentiary weight. The investigator must consult sources across multiple tiers, not merely within a single one.
*   **Tier 1 — Official Government Records:** Electoral registers, land registries, court filings, company registrars (e.g., Companies House), births/deaths/marriages registers, and patent offices. These carry the highest evidentiary weight because they are created under statutory obligation and subject to criminal penalties for falsification.
*   **Tier 2 — Verified Commercial Databases:** Credit reference agencies (e.g., Experian, Equifax), corporate intelligence providers (e.g., Bureau van Dijk/Orbis, Dun & Bradstreet), and specialist registries (e.g., shipping databases, sanctions lists). These are systematically compiled and commercially verified but lack the statutory backing of Tier 1.
*   **Tier 3 — Authenticated Social Media and Self-Published Content:** LinkedIn profiles, personal websites, verified social media accounts, and public statements. The information is directly attributable to the subject but is self-reported and therefore susceptible to fabrication or omission.
*   **Tier 4 — Secondary Commentary and News Aggregation:** News articles, blog posts, forum discussions, and aggregated databases that repackage primary reporting. These sources are most vulnerable to **circular reporting** — the phenomenon where multiple outlets repeat a single original claim, creating a false appearance of independent corroboration.

### 2. The Corroboration Rule
Parallel sourcing is not merely about collecting more data; it is about collecting data from independent origins that cannot share a common root.
*   **Independence Requirement:** Two sources only provide genuine corroboration if they derive their information independently. If Source B learned the fact from Source A, citing both does not constitute parallel sourcing — it constitutes circular reporting.
*   **Cross-Tier Validation:** The strongest corroboration occurs when a claim is supported by sources from different tiers. For example, a directorship claim is strongly corroborated when Companies House filings (Tier 1) align with a LinkedIn profile (Tier 3) and a corporate announcement in a trade publication (Tier 4).
*   **The Three-Source Rule:** For critical claims — those that will materially affect the analytical conclusion or form the basis of a legal finding — a minimum of three independent sources should be sought before the information is graded "1 (Confirmed)" on the Admiralty credibility axis.

### 3. Mapping Tiers to Intelligence Requirements
The ICP from Phase 1 (Task 2.2) defines which Intelligence Requirements (IRs) demand which source types. This mapping must be explicit.
*   **Sequence of Collection:** The plan specifies whether to begin with Tier 1 (official records, slower but authoritative) or Tier 3/4 (open sources, faster but less reliable). In practice, OSINT investigations typically begin with Tier 3/4 to develop initial leads, then validate upward through Tier 1/2.
*   **Source Exhaustion Documentation:** If a tier is unavailable for a specific jurisdiction or entity type (e.g., no public company registry exists in a particular country), this must be documented as a "negative finding" in the **Task Log** (Task 4.2) to demonstrate that all reasonable avenues were pursued.

### 4. Failure Mode: Circular Reporting
The most dangerous failure in parallel sourcing is the illusion of corroboration created by circular reporting.
*   **Detection Method:** The investigator must trace every claim back to its primary source. If multiple Tier 4 articles all cite the same original press release or leaked document, they represent one source, not four.
*   **Information Lineage Mapping:** For high-stakes claims, the investigator should construct a brief lineage diagram showing how the information propagated from its origin through intermediary sources. This lineage is recorded in the **Source Register** (Task 5.3) and prevents artificially inflated credibility ratings.

### 5. Integration with the Admiralty Matrix (Task 3.1b)
The output of parallel sourcing directly informs the credibility axis of the Admiralty grade:
*   Information corroborated across two or more independent tiers may be graded "1 (Confirmed)" or "2 (Probably True)".
*   Information from a single source, regardless of that source's reliability rating, cannot exceed "3 (Possibly True)" unless technically verified through the methods in Task 3.3b.
*   Information where apparent corroboration is traced to circular reporting must be downgraded and the circular dependency flagged in the Source Register.
