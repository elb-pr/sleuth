# Investigation Workflow Guide

This comprehensive workflow guide details the end-to-step investigative process, utilizing a cross-disciplinary architecture designed to ensure professional rigor and auditable logic. The workflow follows a sequential "Tool Dependency Map," where the output of one phase serves as the foundation for the next.

### Phase 1: Operational Direction & Strategic Foundation
The investigation begins with the **Direction** phase, which defines the scope and legal parameters of the inquiry. This is the "foundation" stage that justifies the proportionality of the investigation.

*   **Step 1: Defining Operational Goals.** Use the **Case Decision & Collection Log Template** to identify knowledge gaps and define goals. This template incorporates the **STEEPLES** framework to assess environmental factors and the **PLAN** framework to justify investigative vectors based on proportionality, legality, accountability, and necessity.
*   **Step 2: Strategic Planning.** Develop an **Investigation Plan / Strategy Document**. This dynamic document defines specific intelligence requirements, prioritizes lines of enquiry, and allocates resources for both open and closed source collection.

### Phase 2: Intelligence Collection & Epistemic Filtering
As data is gathered, it must pass through an epistemic filter to prevent "workspace poisoning" by unverified or unreliable information.

*   **Step 3: Initial Vetting.** Apply the **Source & Credibility Grader Template** to every discrete claim entering the intelligence cycle. This utilizes the **Admiralty 6x6 Matrix**, assigning alphanumeric grades (A–F for reliability and 1–6 for credibility) to ensure the historical trustworthiness of the source is assessed independently of the current information.
*   **Step 4: Continuous Logging.** Maintain the **Task Log / Actions Register** to record every investigative enquiry and its specific outcome. It is mandatory to record "negative results" to prove that all reasonable lines of enquiry were pursued.
*   **Step 5: Evidence Preservation.** All physical and digital evidence must be recorded in the **Evidence / Material Register**. This includes cryptographic hash values and capture details to maintain a digital chain of custody.

### Phase 3: Collation & Entity Resolution
In this phase, raw, vetted data is transformed into structured records within a central repository.

*   **Step 6: Structured Extraction.** Use the **Entity Extraction & Profiling Template** to parse raw text into structured records based on **POLE** (Person, Object, Location, Event) schemas. This enforces deterministic matching for unique identifiers like passport numbers.
*   **Step 7: Database Management.** Ingest these records into the **Entity Register**, which serves as the central repository. Every record must maintain strict provenance, including source attribution and the identity of the analyst who last modified it.
*   **Step 8: Specialized Deep Research.** If the case requires it, use specialized templates like the **Genealogical & Family Network Research Template** (following the Genealogical Proof Standard) or the **Cultural Context & Ethnographic Background Template** to map identity structures and power hierarchies.

### Phase 4: Chronological & Relational Processing
Once structured, the data is processed to identify patterns, temporal gaps, and relational hubs.

*   **Step 9: Temporal Normalization.** Use the **Chronological Matrix Template** to take disjointed event data and normalize it into a UTC-standardized timeline. This step is critical for highlighting "unknowns" and resolving contradictions in source timestamps.
*   **Step 10: Relationship Mapping.** Deploy the **Network Architecture (Edge List) Template** to output structured tabular data for link analysis. This is used to identify "Central Hubs" with high connectivity and "Bridge Nodes" that act as gatekeepers between clusters.
*   **Step 11: Media Authentication.** For visual evidence, use the **Content Verification Report** to authenticate photos or videos through reverse image searching, geolocation, and shadow/weather-based chronolocation.

### Phase 5: Hypothesis Reasoning & Cognitive De-biasing
Before a final summary is produced, the findings must undergo a rigorous reasoning process to combat confirmation bias.

*   **Step 12: Testing Alternatives.** The **Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH) Template** acts as a mandatory cognitive checkpoint. It builds a matrix to test evidence against at least three mutually exclusive hypotheses. It operates on the **Inconsistency Principle**, where the most viable explanation is the one with the least evidence against it, rather than the most confirmations.

### Phase 6: Final Reporting & Dissemination
The final stage is the delivery mechanism, designed for rapid consumption by decision-makers while maintaining objective distance.

*   **Step 13: Generating the Core Brief.** Use the **Analytical Briefing Generator Template** to produce a report following **ICD 203** standards. This must feature a **Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF)** structure and strictly separate facts, assumptions, and analytical judgements.
*   **Step 14: Administrative Summary.** Complete the **Case Summary Record**, providing a high-level administrative overview of the case reference, scope, and current status.
*   **Step 15: Practitioner Findings.** Issue a **Findings Memo** to summarize confirmed findings, unverified leads, and scope limitations in a concise format for investigative teams.
