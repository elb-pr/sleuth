Task 11.3, **Spatial Econometrics**, uses the **Economic History Template** to analyse how the economic character of a region or specific site has evolved over time. This task provides the contextual economic intelligence required to assess the plausibility of financial claims, evaluate the economic environment in which entities operate, and identify anomalies that may indicate illicit activity.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Regional Economic Profiling
The analyst constructs an economic profile of the geographic area relevant to the investigation, tracking its evolution across the investigation's time period.
*   **Employment Data:** Historical employment statistics for the region, broken down by sector (industrial, commercial, agricultural, service, public sector). Changes in employment patterns reveal economic transitions that contextualise the activities of investigated entities.
*   **Industry Composition:** The dominant industries in the region and how they have changed. A region transitioning from heavy industry to service economy, for example, provides context for property redevelopment schemes and associated financial flows.
*   **Economic Indicators:** Regional GDP, average income levels, property values, business formation and dissolution rates, and insolvency statistics. These indicators provide the baseline against which individual entity activity can be assessed for anomalies.

### 2. Site-Specific Economic Analysis
Where the investigation focuses on specific properties or business premises, the analyst assesses the economic history and current viability of the site.
*   **Commercial Viability Assessment:** Is the business activity claimed for a specific site economically plausible given the site's location, size, and the regional economic context? A luxury goods retailer in a deprived area, or a logistics company with no transport links, may warrant further investigation.
*   **Property Value Analysis:** Historical property values for the site and comparable properties, identifying transactions that are significantly above or below market value — a common indicator of money laundering through property.
*   **Business Activity Cross-Reference:** Revenue figures claimed by entities operating at the site (from corporate filings) are compared against the regional economic context. A small retail premises claiming revenue orders of magnitude above comparable businesses in the same area is an anomaly flag.

### 3. Significance Assessment
The template outputs a formal significance assessment for each analysed location or region.
*   **Economic Plausibility Rating:** A structured judgment on whether the economic activity associated with the investigated entities is plausible within the regional context. This is not a definitive conclusion but an analytical input for the ACH matrix in Phase 5.
*   **Anomaly Register:** Identified economic anomalies — property transactions at unusual values, revenue claims inconsistent with regional norms, employment patterns that do not match business type — are documented with their source data and fed into the hypothesis testing process.
*   **Contextual Intelligence for Briefing:** The economic context is available for inclusion in the final briefing (Phase 6), where it provides the decision-maker with the background needed to understand why certain financial activities are flagged as suspicious.

### 4. Integration with Broader Investigation
Spatial econometrics does not operate in isolation — it enriches the other analytical streams.
*   **Chronological Matrix (Task 9.1):** Economic events (business incorporations, property transactions, regional economic shocks) are added to the master chronology as contextual events.
*   **Network Analysis (Task 10.1):** Economic relationships (business partnerships, supply chains, property transactions) generate edges in the network graph.
*   **Hypothesis Testing (Phase 5):** Economic plausibility assessments directly inform the ACH matrix. If a hypothesis requires an economically implausible scenario to hold, the plausibility assessment is scored as inconsistent evidence.
