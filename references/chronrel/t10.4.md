Task 10.4, **Two-Mode Analysis**, extends network analysis beyond direct person-to-person relationships to reveal hidden connections through shared affiliations. Standard one-mode networks map entities of the same type (e.g., Person-to-Person). Two-mode networks map entities of different types (e.g., Person-to-Event, Person-to-Organisation, Person-to-Location), exposing indirect affiliations that direct analysis would miss.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. The Two-Mode Network Concept
A two-mode (bipartite) network contains two distinct types of nodes, with edges only connecting nodes of different types.
*   **Mode 1 (Actors):** Typically Person entities — the subjects, associates, and persons of interest in the investigation.
*   **Mode 2 (Affiliations):** Shared contexts — events attended, organisations membership, locations frequented, corporate directorships held, bank accounts accessed, communication platforms used, or vehicles registered to.
*   **Indirect Connection Logic:** If Person A and Person B both hold directorships in Company X, they share a Mode 2 affiliation — even if no direct Person-to-Person relationship has been documented. This co-occurrence is analytically significant: they operate in the same corporate environment and may coordinate without leaving direct communication traces.

### 2. Constructing the Two-Mode Matrix
The analyst selects a specific Mode 2 category based on the investigation's analytical focus and compiles the affiliation data.
*   **Person-to-Event:** Identifies individuals who attended the same meetings, conferences, or incidents. Co-attendance at undisclosed events may reveal hidden coordination.
*   **Person-to-Organisation:** Identifies individuals who share corporate affiliations — directorships, shareholdings, employment. This is the primary method for detecting networks of nominee directors and beneficial ownership structures.
*   **Person-to-Location:** Identifies individuals who frequent the same locations (addresses, jurisdictions, travel destinations). Shared geographic patterns may indicate operational coordination.
*   **Person-to-Account:** Identifies individuals with access to the same financial accounts, phone numbers, or digital resources. Shared account access is a strong indicator of operational trust.

### 3. Projection to One-Mode Networks
The two-mode network can be mathematically projected into a one-mode network to directly map the implied Person-to-Person connections.
*   **Projection Method:** If Person A and Person B both connect to Organisation X in the two-mode network, the projection creates a direct edge between Person A and Person B, weighted by the number of shared affiliations.
*   **Multiplexity:** Individuals who share affiliations across multiple Mode 2 categories (e.g., they are co-directors, attended the same event, and accessed the same bank account) receive higher edge weights in the projected network, indicating a stronger implied connection.
*   **Analytical Caution:** The projected one-mode network represents implied, not proven, relationships. Two individuals who happen to hold directorships in the same large corporation may have no operational connection. The projection generates hypotheses for investigation, not conclusions.

### 4. Identifying Hidden Co-Occurrences
The primary analytical value of two-mode analysis is the detection of relationships that direct evidence collection has not revealed.
*   **Shell Company Networks:** Two-mode Person-to-Organisation analysis commonly exposes networks of individuals who control multiple nominally independent companies. The shared director pattern, invisible in one-mode analysis, becomes immediately apparent when organisations are introduced as Mode 2 nodes.
*   **Event-Based Coordination:** Individuals who systematically appear at the same events without any documented direct communication may be using in-person meetings to avoid digital communication traces. The Person-to-Event two-mode network exposes this pattern.
*   **Geographic Co-Location:** Individuals who independently travel to the same unusual locations (e.g., an obscure jurisdiction with no apparent legitimate purpose) may be coordinating activities that the investigation has not yet identified.

### 5. Integration with SNA Metrics
The projected one-mode network derived from two-mode analysis is subjected to the same SNA metrics applied in Task 10.2.
*   **Hub Detection:** Individuals with many shared affiliations (high degree in the projected network) may be central coordinators.
*   **Bridge Detection:** Individuals who connect otherwise separate clusters of co-affiliated entities (high betweenness in the projected network) may be facilitators or intermediaries.
*   **Visual Output:** The projected network is rendered with the same visual grammar rules defined in Task 10.3, with an additional annotation distinguishing directly evidenced relationships from those inferred through two-mode projection.
