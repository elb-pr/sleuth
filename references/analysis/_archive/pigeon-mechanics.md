# Pigeon Mechanics: Methodology for Causal Belief Analysis

## 1. Scope

This skill identifies beliefs and behaviours that may be maintained by coincidental reinforcement rather than genuine contingency. It is not therapy, diagnosis, or clinical assessment. Extinction tests are proposals — the user decides what to test, what to leave alone, and what to keep believing regardless. Not all pigeon patterns require intervention. Some are benign, adaptive, or simply not worth the cost of testing.

---

## 2. Claim Extraction

When the user makes a statement, identify causal claims:

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Explicit** | "X causes Y" | "Standing desks fixed my back pain" |
| **Post-hoc** | "Since X, Y" | "Since I started meditating, my relationships improved" |
| **Ritual** | "I always X before Y" | "I always review my notes right before — it helps" |
| **Negative** | "Y never works for me" | "Therapy doesn't work for people like me" |
| **Identity** | "I'm just a X person" | "I'm just not a morning person" |

Not every statement contains a causal claim. Technical questions, factual requests, and creative work typically contain none. When no claims are detected, pigeon analysis produces no output.

---

## 3. Wavefunction Analysis

Each identified claim exists in superposition until measured:

**|ψ⟩ = α|causal⟩ + β|pigeon⟩**

Six dimensions shift the probability amplitudes:

| Dimension | High Pigeon Risk | Low Pigeon Risk | Source |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Temporal Contiguity** | Cause immediately preceded effect | Cause and effect separated by mechanism with known latency | Skinner (1948) |
| **Confound Density** | Multiple variables changed simultaneously | Only the believed cause changed | Standard experimental design |
| **Base Rate** | Effect occurs frequently without the believed cause | Effect is rare without the believed cause | Bayesian reasoning; Frederick (2005) |
| **Mechanism Specificity** | No plausible mechanism, or mechanism is "it just works" | Known, specific mechanism connecting cause to effect | Timberlake & Lucas (1985) |
| **Replication** | Person has tested this once or never | Person has tested this repeatedly under varied conditions | Sanchez et al. (2017) |
| **Reversibility** | Removing the believed cause has never been tried | Removing the cause reliably removes the effect | Extinction paradigm (Skinner/Staddon) |

### Scoring

Each dimension scored: **causal** / **uncertain** / **pigeon**

Overall assessment:
- **6 causal:** Strong causal evidence. Probably not pigeon.
- **4-5 causal:** Mostly grounded. Minor pigeon tendencies. Low priority for testing.
- **2-3 causal:** Substantially uncertain. Worth testing if stakes and cost permit.
- **0-1 causal:** Strongly pigeon. Maintained primarily by coincidental reinforcement.

These are probabilistic estimates, not verdicts. "Strongly pigeon" means "the evidence for this belief doesn't hold up well against these six criteria." It does not mean "you are wrong."

---

## 4. Entanglement

Beliefs that share a reinforcement schedule are entangled. Collapsing one affects others.

**Detection:** Two beliefs are entangled when:
- They were formed in the same period or context
- They share supporting evidence (the same anecdote supports both)
- Challenging one triggers emotional defence of the other
- They form a narrative chain ("X led to Y which caused Z")

**Mapping:** Before proposing extinction tests, map entanglement to predict cascade effects. Collapsing a pigeon belief that is entangled with a load-bearing belief (one that supports the user's functioning, identity, or wellbeing) REQUIRES explicit acknowledgment.

**Example:** "I quit sugar and my anxiety went away" may be entangled with "I have control over my mental health" and "Doctors were wrong about me needing medication." Collapsing the sugar-anxiety link could cascade into the other two. The skill maps this before proposing any test.

## 5. Extinction Tests (Collapse Protocols)

An extinction test is the cheapest experiment that would determine whether a belief is causal or pigeon. Design principles:

### 5.1 Test Design
- **Reversibility first:** Can the believed cause simply be removed temporarily? If yes, that's the test.
- **Minimum viable duration:** What's the shortest period that would produce a detectable change?
- **Control for confounds:** What else needs to stay constant during the test?
- **Clear outcome criteria:** What specific, observable result would confirm or disconfirm?

### 5.2 Domain-Specific Patterns

| Domain | Common Pigeon Patterns | Typical Extinction Test |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Health** | "X supplement/diet/habit fixed my Y" | Remove X for 2-4 weeks, monitor Y with same measurement |
| **Career** | "I can only succeed at company/role type X" | Apply to 2-3 opportunities outside believed range, observe results |
| **Relationships** | "People always treat me like X" | Track next 10 interactions, count actual vs predicted |
| **Productivity** | "I can only work under conditions X" | Work under different conditions for 5 sessions, compare output |
| **Finance** | "I'm just not good with money" | Track spending for 30 days with zero intervention, compare to belief |
| **Identity** | "I'm a X person / not a Y person" | Attempt Y under low-stakes conditions 3 times, observe actual performance vs predicted |

### 5.3 Cost-Benefit Check
Before proposing a test, assess:
- **Cost of testing:** Time, effort, risk, discomfort, potential loss
- **Cost of remaining pigeon:** Missed opportunities, distorted decisions, maintained avoidance, resource waste
- **If cost of testing > cost of remaining pigeon:** The rational choice may be to stay pigeon. Say so explicitly.

## 6. Session Analysis

### What Gets Tracked Per Session
- Number of causal claims detected
- Per-claim wavefunction assessment
- Claims confirmed causal (≥4 dimensions scored causal)
- Claims assessed as pigeon (≤1 dimension scored causal)
- Claims remaining in superposition (2-3 dimensions scored causal)
- Entanglement relationships between claims
- Extinction tests proposed
- Domain-level pigeon density (which areas of life show highest false-positive rate)

### Cross-Session Patterns
Over multiple sessions, the profile builds a per-domain d' and β:
- **d' (sensitivity):** How well the user distinguishes genuine causation from correlation in each domain. Calculated from the ratio of confirmed causal beliefs to total beliefs in that domain.
- **β (criterion):** How much evidence the user requires before accepting a causal claim. Inferred from how readily they form causal attributions and how resistant they are to alternative explanations.

### Session Summary Output
At session end, when pigeon patterns were detected, a summary is presented showing:
- Claims analysed with per-claim assessment
- Domain-level pigeon density
- Proposed extinction tests with cost-benefit
- Entanglement warnings where applicable
- Profile sections updated with evidence tier

The summary is delivered in the skill's voice — direct, specific, grounded, with appropriate levity where the stakes are low and appropriate seriousness where they are not.

## 7. Pigeon Severity Scale

Used in session summaries. Calibrated to stakes and evidence quality.

| Level | Label | Criteria | Tone |
|-------|-------|----------|------|
| 🟢 | **Grounded** | ≥4 dimensions causal. Belief is well-supported. | Confirmatory |
| 🟡 | **Superposition** | 2-3 dimensions causal. Uncertain. Worth monitoring. | Curious |
| 🟠 | **Likely Pigeon** | 1 dimension causal. Weak evidence for believed causation. | Direct |
| 🔴 | **Full Pigeon** | 0 dimensions causal. No evidence supports the causal claim. | Firm but respectful |

The scale maps to emojis in casual contexts: 🐦 per pigeon level (🟡 = 🐦, 🟠 = 🐦🐦🐦, 🔴 = 🐦🐦🐦🐦🐦). Used only when rapport is established and the user has opted into the playful framing.

| S12 Behavioural Defaults | Default action under uncertainty determines which pigeons form | Novel pigeon patterns reveal previously unobserved defaults |
| S13 Contingency Sensitivity | This IS the pigeon section — cumulative pigeon findings populate it directly | Every session updates d' and β estimates |
| S15 Signal Discrimination | Low anomaly sensitivity maintains pigeon beliefs | Willingness to examine pigeons reveals epistemic style |
