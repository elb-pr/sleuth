# Source Verification

Systematic source verification for drill journalism using the SIFT method. Load during VERIFY phase for every source in the evidence log.

---

## The SIFT Method (Adapted)

### S — Stop
Before using ANY information from a source, stop. Assess whether you have sufficient context to evaluate this source's reliability. A claim from r/ukdrill carries different weight from a Crown Court judgment. Recognise your own assumptions about the source before proceeding.

### I — Investigate the Source
Who produced this information? What is their relationship to the case?

**For institutional sources:**
- Publication: What editorial standards apply? (BBC, PA, Guardian = established editorial process. Blog, community site = variable)
- Author: Named journalist? Court reporter present? Wire copy?
- Track record: Has this outlet covered drill/gang cases accurately before?
- Institutional motivation: Police press releases serve policing narratives. CPS serves prosecution. Defence solicitors serve clients.

**For community sources (Reddit, Twitter, Instagram):**
- Account age and consistency: Established contributor or new account?
- Posting history: Does this account demonstrate sustained, accurate knowledge of the drill scene?
- Factional alignment: Does the account consistently represent one side of a beef?
- Anonymity level: Named individual, consistent pseudonym, or throwaway?

**For drill music sources:**
- Artist's gang affiliation: Documented? Self-declared? Which set?
- Relationship to the incident: Direct participant? Affiliate? Observer?
- Performance vs testimony: Drill lyrics are performance. They encode real events but through a lens of mythologisation and cultural capital. Distinguish artistic expression from factual testimony.

### F — Find Better Coverage
Before accepting any claim from a single source, search for independent coverage of the same event.

**Independence hierarchy:**
1. Court documents (sentencing remarks, CPS press releases) — highest independence
2. National press court reporting (BBC, PA) — independent observation
3. Local press reporting — may be independent or derived from wire copy
4. Community sources (Reddit, Twitter) — frequently interdependent, often trace to the same origin

**The trace test:** If two sources cite the same underlying document, interview, or social media post, they are ONE source. Count independent origins, not republications.

### T — Trace Claims
Find the original source of every claim. Follow the citation chain backward until you reach the primary source.

**Common citation chains in drill journalism:**
- Reddit thread → Twitter post → Instagram story → eyewitness/participant (often lost)
- News article → police press release → investigating officer (named or unnamed)
- BBC report → PA wire copy → court reporter present at proceedings
- Blog post → news article → police statement (three degrees of separation)
- Drill lyrics → real incident → court documents (requires cross-referencing)

**When the chain breaks:** If the original source cannot be identified, the claim is UNVERIFIED regardless of how many secondary sources repeat it. Repetition is not corroboration.

---

## Verification Workflow

For each source in the evidence log:

1. Apply SIFT assessment
2. Record source type and starting certainty from references/grade-drill-journalism.md
3. Note institutional motivation and narrative lens
4. Check for independent corroboration
5. Trace claims to primary origin
6. Flag any source that fails SIFT (broken chain, untraceable origin, factional bias without counterbalance)

---

## Red Flags

Sources that REQUIRE additional scrutiny:
- Anonymous tips with high specificity (may be genuine, may be deliberate misinformation)
- Sources that emerged AFTER media coverage began (may be attention-seeking, not independent)
- Claims that align perfectly with one faction's narrative without any contradicting detail
- Sources that claim exclusive knowledge but provide details available in published coverage
- Social media posts that appear timed to influence legal proceedings
- Drill lyrics released immediately after an incident (may encode real events OR may be clout-riding)
