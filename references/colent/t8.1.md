Task 8.1, **Developing Subject and Corporate Profiles**, consolidates the resolved entity records from the Entity Register into high-level analytical products that support decision-making. Where the Entity Register stores discrete, field-level data points, the profiles synthesise these into coherent narratives or structured summaries that expose patterns, risks, and gaps. This task is executed using the **Subject & Corporate Profiles Template**.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Subject Profiles (Individuals)
Subject profiles focus on natural persons who are targets, subjects of interest, or key associates within the investigation.
*   **Identity Section:** Full name, known aliases, date of birth, nationality, physical description, and identification document references. All entries cite their source via the Source Register.
*   **Employment and Professional History:** Chronological record of known employment, directorships, professional memberships, and institutional affiliations. Gaps in employment history are flagged as potential knowledge gaps for further collection.
*   **Digital Footprint:** Social media accounts (with platform-specific identifiers, not just display names), email addresses, domain registrations, and any attributed online activity. The attribution evidence (from Task 3.3b Content Verification or Task 6.3 deterministic matching) is cited.
*   **Financial Summary:** Known bank accounts, property holdings, vehicle registrations, corporate shareholdings, and any financial interests identified during collection. This section identifies assets that may be relevant to asset recovery, sanctions compliance, or money laundering investigations.
*   **Relational Summary:** A narrative or tabular summary of the subject's known associates, family members, and professional contacts — drawn from the relationship links in the Entity Register. The strength and source of each association is noted.
*   **Risk Indicators:** Any Politically Exposed Person (PEP) status, sanctions list matches, adverse media findings, or criminal record checks. Each indicator cites its source and the date of the check.

### 2. Corporate Profiles (Organisations)
Corporate profiles focus on legal entities — companies, partnerships, trusts, foundations, and other organisational structures.
*   **Corporate Identity:** Legal name, trading names, registration number, jurisdiction of incorporation, registered address, and incorporation date. For dissolved entities, the dissolution date and circumstances are recorded.
*   **Ownership Chain Mapping:** The critical analytical component. The profile maps the chain of ownership from the entity upward to its ultimate beneficial owner (UBO), identifying each intermediate holding entity, the percentage of ownership at each level, and the jurisdiction of each entity in the chain.
*   **Beneficial Ownership Identification:** Where ownership structures are designed to obscure the UBO (through nominee directors, bearer shares, trusts, or circular ownership), the profile documents the structural techniques employed and assesses the confidence level of UBO attribution.
*   **Circular Ownership Detection:** The profile specifically checks for circular ownership patterns — where Entity A owns Entity B, which owns Entity C, which owns Entity A — as these are red flags for money laundering, tax evasion, or sanctions circumvention.
*   **Director and Officer Analysis:** A list of current and historical directors, officers, and persons of significant control, cross-referenced against the Subject Profiles to identify individuals who appear across multiple entities.
*   **Financial Risk Assessment:** Revenue history, filing compliance, audit opinions, sanctions exposure, and any anomalies identified during financial data collection (e.g., year-on-year revenue variance exceeding 20%, revenue-versus-cash-flow divergence, Altman Z-Score computation for insolvency risk).

### 3. Synthesis Standards
Profiles are not raw data dumps — they are analytical products that require synthesis.
*   **Narrative Coherence:** The profile tells a story — who is this person or entity, what do they do, how are they connected to the investigation, and what are the outstanding questions? Raw field data is woven into an intelligible narrative.
*   **Gap Documentation:** Every profile must include a "Knowledge Gaps" section explicitly listing what information is missing and what additional Intelligence Requirements would need to be fulfilled to complete the profile. This feeds back into the ICP (Task 2.2) if further collection is warranted.
*   **Confidence Transparency:** The profile's confidence level is explicitly stated, derived from the aggregate Admiralty grades of its underlying source material. A profile built primarily on Tier 1 government records carries higher confidence than one built on Tier 3/4 social media and news sources.

### 4. Integration with Downstream Phases
Subject and corporate profiles serve as primary inputs to both the relational processing in Phase 4 and the hypothesis testing in Phase 5.
*   **Network Analysis (Phase 4):** The relational summaries in each profile feed the edge list generation in Task 10.1, where individual associations are compiled into network-level structures.
*   **Chronological Analysis (Phase 4):** Employment histories, directorship tenures, and transaction dates feed the master chronology in Task 9.1.
*   **ACH Matrix (Phase 5):** Profile findings — particularly ownership chains, financial risk indicators, and relational patterns — become evidence rows in the hypothesis testing matrix.
