Task 8.2, **Invoking Specialised Research Templates**, addresses investigations that require contextual intelligence beyond what standard entity profiling can provide. When the subject operates within unfamiliar cultural, genealogical, or geographic contexts, standard POLE extraction and corporate profiling may miss critical relational structures and power dynamics that govern the subject's behaviour. This task deploys two specialised templates to enrich the profiles developed in Task 8.1.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Genealogical and Family Network Research Template
This template is invoked when the investigation involves kinship-based control structures, inheritance disputes, dynastic business ownership, or any context where family relationships are operationally significant.

**Genealogical Proof Standard (GPS):**
*   The template applies the **Genealogical Proof Standard** — an established evidential framework requiring: a reasonably exhaustive search of relevant sources, a complete and accurate citation of each source, analysis and correlation of the collected information, resolution of conflicting evidence, and a soundly reasoned written conclusion.
*   **Kinship Mapping:** The output is a structured family network linking individuals through biological, marital, and adoptive relationships. Each relationship is typed (parent-child, sibling, spouse, in-law) and sourced.
*   **Inheritance and Succession Analysis:** For investigations involving dynastic wealth, the template maps inheritance patterns to identify how control of assets, companies, or land transfers across generations. This is critical for identifying beneficial ownership in jurisdictions where family trusts or customary land tenure obscure formal ownership.

**Hypothesis Testing for Relationships:**
*   Claimed relationships are treated as hypotheses to be tested, not facts to be recorded. The template requires the investigator to seek both confirming and disconfirming evidence for each claimed family link.
*   **DNA and Documentary Evidence:** Where available, biological relationships may be verified through genetic testing results. Documentary evidence includes birth certificates, marriage certificates, parish records, census data, and immigration records.
*   **Naming Pattern Analysis:** In cultures with patronymic, matronymic, or clan-based naming conventions, naming patterns provide circumstantial evidence for kinship claims. The template captures the relevant naming convention rules and applies them to the entity register.

### 2. Cultural Context and Ethnographic Background Template
This template is invoked when the investigation operates across cultural, ethnic, or geographic boundaries where the investigator lacks contextual understanding of local norms, power structures, or social organisation.

**Power Hierarchy Mapping:**
*   The template captures the formal and informal power structures within the relevant community or region. Formal structures include governmental hierarchies, clan or tribal leadership, and religious authority. Informal structures include patronage networks, business oligarchies, and diasporic community leadership.
*   **Community Norms and Taboos:** The investigator documents social norms that may affect how intelligence is collected, interpreted, or verified. For example, gender-segregated social spaces may affect the accessibility of human sources; communal property ownership may not be reflected in individual land registry searches.

**Contextual Risk Assessment:**
*   **Cultural Bias Mitigation:** The template forces the investigator to identify and document their own assumptions about the community or culture under investigation. This explicit self-assessment mitigates the risk of projecting familiar cultural frameworks onto unfamiliar contexts, which can lead to misinterpretation of behaviour, relationships, or motivations.
*   **Communication and Language Factors:** Language barriers, dialectal variation, and culturally specific terminology are documented. Translation requirements and the reliability of available translators are assessed and recorded.
*   **Safety Considerations:** The template captures any safety risks associated with conducting research in or about the relevant community — including risks to the investigator, risks to sources, and risks to the subjects of investigation if the inquiry becomes known.

### 3. Integration with Entity Profiles and Downstream Phases
The outputs of both specialised templates are not standalone products — they are enrichment layers that feed back into the core investigative workflow.
*   **Entity Register Enrichment:** Family relationships identified through the Genealogical Template are added to the Entity Register as typed links (e.g., `FAMILY_OF`, `HEIR_TO`, `SPOUSE_OF`) with full source attribution and GPS compliance documentation.
*   **Network Analysis (Phase 4):** Kinship networks and power hierarchies feed the network visualisation in Task 10.1, revealing structural patterns (e.g., a family controlling multiple nominally independent companies) that would be invisible in standard corporate profiling.
*   **Hypothesis Testing (Phase 5):** Cultural context informs the framing of hypotheses in the ACH matrix. A hypothesis about a subject's motivations must account for cultural norms that the investigator may not share — the ethnographic template provides this contextual grounding.
*   **Final Briefing (Phase 6):** Any cultural context critical to understanding the analytical conclusions must be surfaced in the briefing, with appropriate caveats about the investigator's level of cultural expertise.
