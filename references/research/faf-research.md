# Friends, Associates & Family Research

How to investigate the network around a subject. Adapted from genealogical FAN methodology for gang investigation.

---

## The FAN Principle

Research the network around your subject, not just the subject.

**Family:**
- Parents, siblings, partners, children
- Extended family appearing in court records or news
- Family members with their own gang affiliations or criminal history
- Family addresses (current and historical - reveals movement patterns)
- Family members who have spoken publicly (media, social media, court testimony)

**Associates:**
- Co-defendants in court proceedings (gold for mapping operational connections)
- Musical collaborators (featuring credits, producer connections)
- Social media connections (tagged in photos, mentioned in posts, appearing in stories)
- Known associates named in sentencing remarks
- People who appear alongside the subject in multiple independent contexts

**Neighbours:**
- Other residents of the same estate
- People from the same postcode appearing in court for similar offences
- Youth workers, school staff, community leaders who knew the subject
- Local business owners
- Other gangs or groups operating in the same geographic area

**Why it works:** The subject is rarely the most informative source about themselves. Their network reveals connections, patterns, and context that direct investigation of the subject alone cannot provide.

## Network Research Patterns

### Circle Mapping

Start with the subject at the centre. Build outward in concentric circles:

**Inner circle (high confidence, direct evidence):**
- Named co-defendants
- Named family members in court documents
- Confirmed gang members from the same set

**Middle circle (medium confidence, indirect evidence):**
- People appearing in the same social media posts
- Musical collaborators
- People from the same estate of similar age
- Named associates in news coverage

**Outer circle (low confidence, inferred):**
- People mentioned in the same Reddit threads
- People from allied gangs
- People with similar social media activity patterns

### Relationship Inference

When direct evidence of a relationship does not exist, look for:
- **Co-occurrence:** Same event, same location, same timeframe (court, social media, news)
- **Shared affiliations:** Same gang, same school, same estate, same music label
- **Network bridging:** Two people who both connect to the same third person
- **Temporal patterns:** Social media activity at similar times, appearing in each other's content

All inferred relationships MUST be marked with confidence level. An inference is a hypothesis, not a finding.

### Indirect Signals

Sometimes the strongest evidence about a relationship comes from absence or indirect markers:
- Subject removes photos with someone after an incident (relationship existed but is being concealed)
- Co-defendant turns prosecution witness (relationship breakdown)
- Family member makes public statement distancing themselves (loyalty fracture)
- Drill track that previously featured collaborations is re-released without certain artists (alliance shift)

## Adapted Genealogical Methods

### Source Classification for Network Evidence

From the genealogical evidence framework, adapted:

**Original sources** - first recording of the relationship:
- Court documents naming co-defendants
- Birth/marriage records linking family members
- Official gang intelligence (if accessible)

**Derivative sources** - copies or references to the original:
- News articles reporting co-defendant relationships
- Reddit summaries of court proceedings
- Database entries in gang knowledge bases

**Authored works** - compiled or interpreted:
- Community accounts of who is affiliated with whom
- Drill commentary channels explaining gang connections
- Academic research on specific gang networks

### Evidence Weight for Relationships

Quality over quantity. One court document naming two people as co-defendants in a specific offence outweighs ten Reddit posts saying they are associated.

**Strongest evidence:**
- Co-defendant status in court proceedings
- Named family relationship in official records
- Direct visual evidence (geotagged photo together at a specific location)

**Medium evidence:**
- Consistent social media interaction patterns
- Multiple independent community sources agreeing
- Musical collaboration with shared gang references

**Weakest evidence (still worth noting, but needs corroboration):**
- Single Reddit post claiming association
- Shared postcode without other connection
- Similar age and estate without direct link

### Cross-Validation

For any claimed relationship, seek evidence from at least two independent source types:
- Court records + social media
- News coverage + community accounts
- Drill music references + geographic co-location

Two sources from the same type (e.g., two Reddit posts) do not count as independent corroboration. They may share an upstream origin.

## Investigating Family Context

Family context matters for understanding how someone ended up where they are. This is not about blame - it is about understanding the environment that shaped decisions.

**What to look for:**
- Family stability or disruption during key developmental years
- Parental involvement with criminal justice system
- Sibling involvement in the same or rival gangs
- Family connections to the estate (how long, how deep)
- Protective factors (family members who provided stability, mentors)
- Family response to the subject's involvement (support, distance, conflict)

**Where to find it:**
- Sentencing remarks often include family background
- Pre-sentence reports (sometimes quoted in sentencing remarks)
- News coverage of family statements during/after trial
- Community accounts of family reputation on the estate
- Social media activity of family members

**Handle with care:** Family members are often the most affected and least responsible. Profile family context to understand the subject, not to implicate the family.

## Practical Workflow

When you need to investigate someone's network:

1. Start with the subject's court history. Extract every co-defendant name.
2. Run each co-defendant through the same process (court history, social media, drill connections).
3. Build the FAN map outward from the subject.
4. Cross-reference against the gang knowledge base for known affiliations.
5. Use geo data to check whether people from the network cluster geographically.
6. Look for bridges - people who connect otherwise separate groups.
7. Identify absent voices - who is affected but not represented in any source?

Every step produces questions for the next step. The network reveals itself iteratively.
