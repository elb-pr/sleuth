### Tier 2: Decision Framework per Method

Each method in the handbook follows a consistent eight-part decision structure:

1. **What is the method?** - Definition and scope
2. **When should it be used?** - Trigger conditions and appropriateness
3. **What do I need to consider?** - Design variables (population, sampling, format, questions, administration)
4. **How should it be used?** - Delivery mechanism and practical deployment
5. **What is the output?** - Data type produced (numerical, textual, visual, audio)
6. **How should it be analysed?** - Tools and techniques (Excel, SPSS, NVivo, NUD*IST, Transana)
7. **Pros and Cons** - Comparative advantages and limitations
8. **Further Reading** - External resources and training

## PART 2: Thinking Toolkit Skills Mapped to Research Methodology

Each of our 12 thinking tools maps to specific research phases, challenges, and methodological enhancements. This is not a loose analogy; these are structural isomorphisms where the tool's mechanism directly solves a known research problem.

### Research Phase Mapping

| Research Phase | Primary Stuck-ness | Thinking Tool | Mechanism of Enhancement |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Problem Definition** | "Am I researching the right question?" | Cause-Effect Confusion | The Symptom Test validates whether the research question targets root phenomena or surface symptoms. The XY Problem detection prevents researching solutions-in-disguise rather than actual problems. |
| **Research Design** | "Does the sequence of methods matter?" | Temporal Blindness | Tests whether Method A before Method B produces different findings than B before A. Identifies irreversible design commitments and path dependencies in longitudinal studies. |
| **Hypothesis Generation** | "I need a genuinely novel hypothesis" | Collision-Zone Thinking | Forces cross-domain conceptual collisions to generate hypotheses invisible within a single discipline. Treats "What if we studied X as if it were Y?" as a generative research strategy. |
| **Assumption Audit** | "What are we taking for granted?" | Inversion Exercise | Flips every core assumption in the research design and asks what would change if the opposite were true. Surfaces protected beliefs masquerading as methodology. |
| **Literature Synthesis** | "I keep seeing the same pattern across studies" | Meta-Pattern Recognition | Extracts universal principles from cross-domain convergence in existing literature. Distinguishes true universals from coincidental similarities. Tests predictive power of identified patterns. |
| **Scale & Generalisability** | "Will these findings hold at scale?" | Scale Game | Tests findings at 1000x and 1/1000x scale to reveal boundary conditions. Exposes what holds universally versus what is an artefact of comfortable sample sizes. |
| **Methodology Simplification** | "This research design is getting too complex" | Simplification Cascades | Identifies the one insight that eliminates multiple methodological components. Finds where apparent complexity masks a simpler underlying structure. |
| **Stakeholder Perspectives** | "Different groups interpret findings differently" | Perspective Mapping | Models how different stakeholders (participants, funders, policymakers, communities) experience and interpret the same data. Surfaces whose optimisation function the research serves. |
| **Conflicting Evidence** | "Study A says X, Study B says not-X" | Contradiction Holding | Refuses premature resolution. Classifies contradictions as false dichotomy, context-dependent, scale-dependent, temporal, or true paradox. Extracts insight from the tension itself. |
| **Systemic Research Problems** | "Our corrections keep creating new problems" | Feedback Loop Mapping | Maps circular causality in research systems. Identifies where methodological interventions feed back into the system being studied. Reveals where researcher is inside the loop. |
| **Research Prioritisation** | "Too many questions, limited resources" | Priority Paralysis | Breaks value-flattening deadlock in research agenda setting. Identifies the ONE research question that makes others easier or unnecessary. |
| **Go/No-Go Decisions** | "Not enough data to decide on methodology" | Decision Paralysis | Calculates minimum confidence needed to commit to a research design. Distinguishes reversible from irreversible methodological choices. |

### Integration Architecture

Each tool integrates into the research process at specific decision nodes:

```
RESEARCH LIFECYCLE
│
├─► QUESTION FORMULATION
│   ├── Cause-Effect Confusion → Validate question targets root cause
│   ├── Inversion Exercise → Stress-test assumptions in question
│   └── Priority Paralysis → Select from competing questions
│
├─► DESIGN PHASE
│   ├── Temporal Blindness → Sequence methods correctly
│   ├── Scale Game → Validate sample and generalisability
│   ├── Simplification Cascades → Streamline methodology
│   └── Decision Paralysis → Commit to design under uncertainty
│
├─► DATA COLLECTION
│   ├── Perspective Mapping → Account for stakeholder viewpoints
│   ├── Feedback Loop Mapping → Detect researcher-as-variable effects
│   └── Cause-Effect Confusion → Monitor for XY problems emerging in fieldwork
│
├─► ANALYSIS
│   ├── Meta-Pattern Recognition → Cross-domain synthesis
│   ├── Contradiction Holding → Handle conflicting findings
│   └── Scale Game → Test if findings hold at extremes
│
├─► INTERPRETATION & REPORTING
│   ├── Perspective Mapping → Frame for multiple audiences
│   ├── Collision-Zone Thinking → Generate novel interpretations
│   └── Feedback Loop Mapping → Map systemic implications
│
└─► META-RESEARCH (researching the research process itself)
    ├── All 12 tools available as diagnostic instruments
    └── Thinking Toolkit as router for research stuck-ness
```

- Real-time adaptive research protocols

### E. Research Integrity in AI Context
- Bias propagation in AI-assisted research

## PART 4: The Research Prompt

The following prompt is designed to be executed by another Claude instance with web search enabled. It will conduct the research needed to populate the AI Research Methods Handbook.

---

```
<system>
You are a senior research methodologist conducting a comprehensive literature and practice review to inform the creation of an AI-Native Research Methods Handbook. This handbook will be used by AI systems and AI-augmented human researchers to conduct social research in 2026 and beyond.

Your task is to conduct thorough web-based research across six domains, synthesise findings into structured output, and identify gaps requiring further investigation. You will search the web extensively, reading full articles where necessary, and compile your findings into a structured research report.

IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS:
- Search extensively. Use 5-10+ searches per domain. Follow leads.
- Fetch and read full articles when snippets are insufficient.
- Prioritise primary sources: academic papers, institutional frameworks, official guidelines, practitioner reports.
- Distinguish between established consensus, emerging practice, and speculative/theoretical claims.
- Flag contradictions explicitly rather than resolving them prematurely.
- Use British spelling throughout.
- Structure all output using the section framework below.
- When you find conflicting information, note both positions and the strength of evidence for each.
</system>

<task>
Conduct research across the following six domains. For each domain, search the web for current (2024-2026) information, read relevant sources in full, and compile findings using the output structure specified.
```
