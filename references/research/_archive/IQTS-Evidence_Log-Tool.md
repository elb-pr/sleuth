## EVIDENCE LOG TOOL

Purpose: The Evidence Log Tool provides documentation and details on all evidence collected
for the investigation, by whom, and when. The evidence log is part of the Structured Principle
in sexual exploitation, abuse and harassment (SEAH) investigations, ensuring investigations are
thorough, appropriately documented, and process-oriented.

How to use this tool: When gathering evidence, investigators must document every piece of
evidence they obtain; where it was obtained; and diligently document who collected it, who
logged it, what the evidence is, and provide a unique evidence ID number and the date and
signature of the person who logged the evidence. It is important that investigators document
evidence as soon as it is gathered. More information on this tool can be found in section 3.2.1 of
the Sexual Exploitation, Abuse and Harassment (SEAH) Investigation Guide.

Definition of key components

- Name of person collecting the evidence: Refers to the name of the investigator or individual
who obtained the evidence.

- Name of person logging the evidence: Refers to the name of the investigator or individual
responsible for documenting the evidence in the evidence log.

- Remarks: Includes any important details about the evidence such as where it was obtained, who
provided the evidence, what kind of evidence it is, etc.

- Evidence ID number: Every piece of evidence needs to be given a unique ID number and the
evidence labeled with this ID number.
