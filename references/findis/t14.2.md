
Task 14.2, **Maintaining the Dissemination Log**, creates the structured audit trail recording exactly who received the investigation's outputs, what material was shared, and what restrictions govern further distribution. This log is the final accountability mechanism — it ensures that intelligence products are traceable from production to consumption.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Mandatory Fields per Dissemination Entry
Each act of sharing intelligence products is recorded with the following:
*   **Dissemination Reference Number:** A unique sequential identifier for each distribution event.
*   **Date and Time of Dissemination:** When the material was shared, timestamped to UTC.
*   **Recipient Identity:** The specific individual or organisation that received the material. In law enforcement contexts, this may include rank, role, and agency.
*   **Material Shared:** Exactly which documents, datasets, or briefing products were provided. This is identified by the document's case file reference number, not a general description.
*   **Classification and Handling Restrictions:** Any sensitivity markings, compartmentalisation rules, or handling codes (e.g., 5×5×5 handling code in UK policing) attached to the disseminated material.
*   **Authorising Officer:** The identity of the individual who authorised the dissemination, confirming that it was within the Terms of Reference and handling restrictions.
*   **Purpose of Dissemination:** Why the material was shared — for action, for information, for legal proceedings, for supervisory review, etc.

### 2. Restrictions on Further Dissemination
The log must record any conditions placed on the recipient's use of the material.
*   **Third-Party Distribution Prohibition:** Whether the recipient is authorised to share the material further, and if so, with whom.
*   **Source Protection Restrictions:** If the material contains information derived from protected sources (e.g., covert human intelligence sources), restrictions preventing the disclosure of source identities must be recorded and communicated to the recipient.
*   **Redaction Requirements:** If the material was provided in a redacted form (e.g., with source identities removed, or with specific intelligence methods concealed), the redactions and their reasons are documented.

### 3. Legal Disclosure Tracking
In investigations that may lead to legal proceedings, the Dissemination Log forms part of the disclosure record.
*   **Prosecution Disclosure:** Material shared with prosecution teams must be logged with specific reference to the disclosure rules (e.g., CPIA in UK). The log must record whether the material constitutes "used" or "unused" material in the prosecution file.
*   **Defence Disclosure:** If material is disclosed to the defence (as required by CPIA for material that might undermine the prosecution or assist the defence), this is logged separately with the specific legal basis for disclosure.
*   **Sensitive Material Schedules:** Material that cannot be disclosed to the defence due to public interest immunity or source protection is recorded on separate schedules (e.g., MG6 forms in UK), and the existence (but not content) of these schedules is logged in the Dissemination Log.

### 4. Audit and Integrity
The Dissemination Log is itself subject to the same integrity standards as other case file components.
*   **Contemporaneous Recording:** Entries must be made at the time of dissemination, not retrospectively.
*   **Immutability:** Once an entry is recorded, it cannot be altered. Corrections are made by adding a new entry referencing the original.
*   **Archival:** The completed log is archived alongside the Evidence Register and Source Register as part of the permanent case file.
