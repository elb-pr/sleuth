Task 15.1, **Issuing the Findings Memo**, produces the practitioner-facing dissemination product for investigative teams. Where the Analytical Briefing (Step 13) is designed for senior decision-makers and meets international intelligence standards, the Findings Memo is designed for field practitioners, junior analysts, and investigative colleagues who require a concise, operational summary of what the investigation established, what it did not establish, and where the boundaries of the inquiry lie.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Purpose and Audience Distinction
The Findings Memo is not a condensed version of the Analytical Briefing — it is a structurally distinct product serving a different function.
*   **Audience:** Field investigators, lead analysts, or case officers who need to act on the investigation's outputs. They require precise, unambiguous statements of what was confirmed, what remains unresolved, and what falls outside the scope of the inquiry.
*   **Operational Orientation:** The Findings Memo emphasises actionable findings over analytical narrative. Where the Analytical Briefing argues a conclusion, the Findings Memo states confirmed facts, flags outstanding leads, and signals investigative gaps requiring follow-up.
*   **Scope Limitation Transparency:** Unlike executive briefings, which may reference limitations briefly, the Findings Memo makes scope constraints explicit and prominent. Practitioners must understand the precise boundaries within which the findings are valid.

### 2. Standard Structure
The Findings Memo follows a fixed structure to ensure consistency across investigations.
*   **Case Reference and Header:** Case reference number, investigation title, issuing analyst, date of issue, and handling classification.
*   **Confirmed Findings:** A numbered list of findings that have been verified to the evidentiary standard established in Phase 2. Each entry carries a cross-reference to the Evidence Register and the relevant Admiralty credibility grade. Only findings graded 1 (Confirmed) or 2 (Probably True) on the information credibility axis appear in this section.
*   **Corroborated Findings:** Findings supported by multiple sources that have not reached confirmed status — typically because independent corroboration exists but the primary source reliability is below A or B on the Admiralty axis. Each entry is marked with its corroboration status (e.g., "Two independent sources, neither formally authenticated").
*   **Unverified Leads:** Specific lines of enquiry that were identified but not resolved within the investigation's scope. Each entry states what the lead is, what source generated it, and the recommended follow-up action. These are not findings — they are tasking recommendations for future investigation.
*   **Negative Results:** Explicitly documented lines of enquiry that were pursued and produced no substantive result. Per Task 4.2, negative results must be recorded to demonstrate that reasonable lines of enquiry were exhausted. Each entry states what was searched, under what parameters, and why the absence of results is significant or expected.
*   **Scope Limitations:** A precise statement of what the investigation did not cover and why. This section prevents practitioners from over-extending the memo's findings into areas the investigation was not designed to address. Causes of scope limitation include: resource constraints, legal access restrictions, jurisdictional gaps, or deliberate scoping decisions recorded in the Policy File.
*   **Conclusions and Assessment:** A brief analytical synthesis connecting the confirmed and corroborated findings to the investigation's original Intelligence Requirements. This section uses ICD 203 probability language for any probabilistic claim. It must reference the Analysis of Alternatives from Phase 5 to demonstrate that competing explanations were considered.
*   **Source Index:** A numbered list of all sources referenced in the memo, linked to their Source Register entries. This enables any recipient to audit the evidential basis of any finding independently.

### 3. Quality Standards
*   **Factual Precision:** Every finding must be stated in falsifiable terms. Vague characterisations ("the subject appeared to have connections to financial networks") are prohibited. Specific, verifiable statements are required ("Subject A holds a directorship in Company B, incorporated in Jurisdiction X on [date], as confirmed by Companies House filing [reference]").
*   **No Conclusions Without Facts:** Conclusions in the final section must be derivable from the confirmed and corroborated findings listed earlier. No inference may appear in the conclusions that does not trace to a specific finding entry.
*   **ICD 203 Compliance:** Any probabilistic language in the conclusions section must comply with the ICD 203 7-tier scale (Task 13.3). Non-standard hedging is prohibited.
*   **Source Completeness:** The Source Index must be exhaustive. No source cited in any finding entry may be absent from the index.
