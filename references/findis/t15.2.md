Task 15.2, **Social Media Attribution (Account Attribution Report)**, produces a specialised dissemination product for investigations in which a social media or digital account requires attribution to a real-world identity. This report does not simply describe what an account posted — it presents, in structured form, the specific evidence chain that supports or refutes the claim that a given account belongs to a specific individual.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Preconditions for Issuing the Report
The Account Attribution Report is only issued when the investigation has produced evidence bearing on digital identity. It is not a generic profile of a social media presence — it is an attribution brief.
*   **Attribution Hypothesis:** The report must be anchored to a specific, testable hypothesis: "Account X belongs to Individual Y." If the investigation has not identified a candidate real-world identity, the report instead documents the account profile and outstanding investigative leads, without an attribution conclusion.
*   **Evidence Threshold:** Attribution conclusions — even at "possible" confidence — must be grounded in at least one piece of independently verifiable evidence. Stylistic similarity or topical overlap alone is insufficient to make an attribution claim.
*   **Chain of Custody:** All digital evidence supporting the attribution must have been preserved in accordance with Task 5.1 (cryptographic hashing) and Task 5.2 (metadata preservation). Unarchived screenshots or undated grabs are inadmissible as attribution evidence in legal or regulatory proceedings.

### 2. Standard Report Structure
*   **Executive Summary:** The account's platform, handle, and attributed identity (if determined), the attribution confidence level using ICD 203 language, and a one-paragraph summary of the evidence chain. The confidence level must distinguish between source reliability and attribution probability — these must not be blended in the same sentence (Task 13.3).
*   **Account Profile:** Full technical profile of the account under investigation — platform, handle/username, display name, profile photograph, biography, account creation date (if recoverable), follower and following counts, approximate posting frequency, and the date range of the archived material examined. Each data point carries a timestamp and archive reference.
*   **Content Analysis:** A thematic and temporal analysis of the account's output. Topics and narrative themes, geographic references and location claims, language and writing style (including recurring vocabulary, syntactical patterns, and colloquialisms), temporal posting patterns (days and hours of activity, which can indicate time zone and operational schedule), and any self-referential disclosures (occupation, relationships, life events). This section draws on the SOCMINT analytical work conducted in Phase 4.
*   **Attribution Evidence:** The structured evidence chain linking the account to the attributed real-world identity. Evidence types are presented in descending order of reliability:
    *   **Unique Identifiers:** Email addresses recovered from account registration, phone numbers linked to the account via platform lookup, biographical data matching government records (date of birth, location, occupation).
    *   **Cross-Platform Matching:** The same handle, profile photograph, writing style, or content appearing on other platforms that carry stronger identity signals (e.g., a LinkedIn profile, a verified news article, a company website).
    *   **Network Connections:** The account's follower/following network overlapping with verified associates of the attributed individual. This is indirect evidence and must be presented as corroboration, not as a primary identifier.
    *   **Content-Based Corroboration:** Posts referencing specific events, places, relationships, or knowledge that only the attributed individual could plausibly possess.
    *   **Technical Evidence:** EXIF metadata recovered from media posted by the account (where not stripped by the platform), IP address indicators (only where legally obtained), and device fingerprinting evidence.
*   **Counter-Attribution Analysis:** Explicit consideration of whether the attribution evidence could be explained by an alternative account holder. Could the account be operated by multiple individuals? Could it be a sockpuppet created to impersonate the attributed individual? Could the stylistic and content similarities be coincidental? This section applies the Analysis of Alternatives logic from Phase 5 to the attribution question.
*   **Confidence Assessment:** A structured statement of attribution confidence, differentiated across the evidence types presented:
    *   **Confirmed:** The account is definitively linked to the individual via a unique, verifiable identifier (e.g., the individual themselves posted a link to the account from a verified channel).
    *   **Probable:** Multiple independent evidence types are consistent with attribution, and no material contradictory evidence exists.
    *   **Possible:** Limited evidence supports attribution, but corroboration is insufficient for a confident conclusion.
    *   **Uncertain:** Evidence exists suggesting a connection but does not support a positive attribution at any confidence level.
*   **Operational and Legal Considerations:** Any handling restrictions on the evidence (e.g., material obtained under a legal access regime that restricts onward disclosure), platform terms of service constraints on republication of account content, and any GDPR or privacy law considerations relevant to the dissemination of the attribution findings.
*   **Source Documentation:** A complete list of archived evidence items, their hash values, archival URLs, and collection dates, cross-referenced to the Evidence Register.

### 3. Quality Standards
*   **Verification Methodology Transparency:** The report must state which verification methods were applied to each evidence item — reverse image search, geolocation, chronolocation, metadata analysis, cross-platform corroboration — consistent with the standards documented in Task 11.1 and the SOCMINT methodology survey.
*   **Archived Evidence Only:** No evidence may be cited in the attribution brief that has not been preserved in the Evidence Register with a valid hash value. Live links to unarchived social media content are not acceptable as standalone evidence.
*   **No Inference Stacking:** Attribution chains must not build multiple inferences on each other without anchoring back to independently verified facts. Each step in the attribution chain must trace to a discrete, sourced evidence item.
