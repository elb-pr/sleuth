
Task 14.3, **Evidence Vault Archiving**, seals the digital chain of custody by transferring all final digital assets from the working Evidence Register into a read-only, access-logged evidence vault. This is the terminal step in evidence management — after archiving, the investigation's digital record is immutable.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Final Hash Verification
Before transfer to the vault, every digital asset undergoes a final integrity check.
*   **Hash Recalculation:** The SHA-256 hash of every file in the Evidence Register is recalculated and compared against the hash recorded at the time of original capture (Task 5.1). Any discrepancy indicates that the file has been altered — intentionally or accidentally — since capture.
*   **Discrepancy Resolution:** If a hash mismatch is detected, the file is quarantined and the discrepancy is investigated. Possible causes include accidental corruption during storage, format conversion, or unauthorised modification. The resolution is documented in the Evidence Register.
*   **Hash Log Compilation:** A final hash log is compiled listing every evidence item, its original hash (from capture), its final hash (pre-archiving), and the verification result (match/mismatch). This log accompanies the vault as the definitive integrity record.

### 2. Vault Transfer
Verified assets are moved to the evidence vault — a storage environment with specific security properties.
*   **Read-Only Access:** Once transferred, files in the vault cannot be modified, deleted, or overwritten. The vault enforces write-once-read-many (WORM) access controls.
*   **Access Logging:** Every access to the vault — including read-only access — is automatically logged with the accessor's identity, timestamp, and the specific files accessed. This audit trail continues indefinitely.
*   **Redundancy:** The vault should maintain redundant copies (at minimum, two geographically separated copies) to protect against physical loss.

### 3. Vault Contents
The evidence vault contains the complete digital record of the investigation.
*   **Primary Evidence:** All collected digital artefacts — screenshots, documents, media files, financial records, communications records — with their original filenames and directory structure preserved.
*   **Derived Analytical Products:** The Entity Register export, Chronological Matrix, Network Analysis outputs (edge lists, SNA metrics, visualisations), ACH Matrix (scored and annotated), and the final Analytical Briefing.
*   **Administrative Records:** The Case Decision Log, Task Log, Source Register, Evidence Register metadata, Dissemination Log, and Case Summary Record.
*   **Hash Log:** The final hash verification log compiled in step 1.

### 4. Access Governance Post-Archiving
The vault's access governance must be defined before archiving is completed.
*   **Access Authority:** Who has the authority to grant access to the archived vault? This is typically the original lead investigator, the supervising officer, or a designated records custodian.
*   **Retention Period:** How long the vault contents must be retained. This varies by jurisdiction and case type — criminal evidence may be retained indefinitely; corporate investigation records may have defined retention periods under data protection law.
*   **Disposal Protocol:** When the retention period expires (if applicable), the disposal method is documented. Cryptographic destruction (secure deletion followed by verification) is the standard for sensitive material.

### 5. Chain of Custody Certification
The archiving process concludes with a formal certification.
*   **Certification Statement:** The lead investigator or evidence custodian certifies that all evidence items have been verified, transferred, and secured in accordance with the investigation's chain of custody standards.
*   **Continuity of Custody:** The certification confirms that the chain of custody is unbroken from the point of original capture (Task 5.1) through the investigation's analytical phases to final archival — the evidence has been accounted for at every stage.
