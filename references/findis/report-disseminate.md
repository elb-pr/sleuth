Phase 6 of the investigative workflow, **Final Reporting & Dissemination**, represents the "Delivery" mechanism of the entire process. This phase synthesizes the structured data from the repository (Phase 3), the patterns identified through relational mapping (Phase 4), and the results of the rigorous hypothesis testing (Phase 5) into high-clarity, objective products tailored for decision-makers.

### Step 13: Synthesizing the Executive Briefing
The primary instrument for high-level delivery is the **Analytical Briefing Generator Template**. This is used when the investigation requires a formal, defensive assessment that meets international intelligence standards.

*   **Task 13.1: Constructing the Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF).** Unlike academic writing, the main analytical message must appear immediately at the beginning of the document. This ensures senior decision-makers can consume the core judgment under time pressure without reading through the narrative build-up.
*   **Task 13.2: Structural Separation of Facts, Assumptions, and Judgments.** To maintain objective distance, the report must use distinct headers to separate:
    *   **Facts (Intelligence):** Verified data points from the repository.
    *   **Assumptions:** Suppositions used to bridge information gaps or frame the argument.
    *   **Judgments:** The final analytical conclusions derived from the synthesis of facts and assumptions.
*   **Task 13.3: Applying Standardized Probability Language.** All probabilistic claims must be mapped to the **ICD 203 7-tier scale**. Terms such as "unlikely" (20–45%), "roughly even chance" (45–55%), and "very likely" (80–95%) must be used strictly.
*   **Task 13.4: Mandatory Source Summary Statement.** Immediately following the BLUF, the report must include a transparent assessment of the quality, credibility, and significant gaps in the underlying source base.

### Step 14: Administrative Finalization and Audit
Parallel to the narrative report, the investigator must finalize the administrative record to ensure the investigation is auditable and legally defensible.

*   **Task 14.1: Finalizing the Case Summary Record.** This high-level administrative overview captures the final status of the case, the reference number, and the definitive scope statement.
*   **Task 14.2: Maintaining the Dissemination Log.** A structured audit trail is created to record the identity of every intelligence recipient, what specific material was shared, and any restrictions placed on further dissemination.
*   **Task 14.3: Evidence Vault Archiving.** Final digital assets from the **Evidence / Material Register** are moved to read-only, access-logged evidence vaults, with a final verification of their SHA-256 cryptographic hash values to ensure the digital chain of custody is sealed.

### Step 15: Specialized and Practitioner Dissemination
Depending on the audience (e.g., field practitioners, legal counsel, or technical teams), specialized templates are deployed to present specific facets of the findings.

*   **Task 15.1: Issuing the Findings Memo.** For investigative teams, a **Findings Memo** is used to summarize confirmed findings, unverified leads, and explicitly state any scope limitations in a concise format.
*   **Task 15.2: Social Media Attribution (Account Attribution Report).** If the investigation involves digital identity, this specialized brief provides a profile of a social media account and the specific evidence supporting its attribution to a real-world identity.
*   **Task 15.3: Strategic and Tactical Assessments (NIM).** Law enforcement investigators use the **Strategic and Tactical Assessments Template** to drive immediate operational tasking or inform long-term force strategy based on current threat patterns.
*   **Task 15.4: Legal Disclosure Preparation (UK MG5 Standard).** If the case is intended for prosecution, the investigator compiles a factual, objective chronological summary of the evidence, stripped of covert tactics or personal opinions to meet disclosure requirements.

### Phase 6 Quality Checkpoints and Failure Mitigations
To prevent analytical failure during the dissemination phase, the following checks are mandatory:
*   **Avoid Confidence/Likelihood Conflation:** Ensure that "confidence" (quality of the evidence) and "likelihood" (probability of the event) are never blended in the same sentence.
*   **Caveat Visibility:** Critical limitations or data gaps must be featured in the main body of the report, not relegated to footnotes or appendices where they may be missed.
*   **Narrative Bias Audit:** Ensure the "Analysis of Alternatives" from Phase 5 is explicitly referenced to prove that contrary data was formally considered.
*   **Intelligence Compartmentalization:** In legal summaries, sensitive intelligence methods must be moved to separate, protected schedules (e.g., MG6 forms) to prevent the accidental disclosure of covert tactics.
