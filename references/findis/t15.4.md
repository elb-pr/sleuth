Task 15.4, **Legal Disclosure Preparation (UK MG5 Standard)**, produces the evidential summary required when an investigation is intended to support criminal prosecution. The MG5 (Police Report) is the standardised form under the UK Manual of Guidance (MG) system through which investigating officers present the case to the Crown Prosecution Service (CPS). Its production is governed by the Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA) and its Code of Practice, which impose specific obligations on investigators regarding what must be disclosed and how it must be documented.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Governing Legal Framework
The CPIA Code of Practice establishes the mandatory obligations that govern this task.
*   **Duty to Pursue All Reasonable Lines of Enquiry:** The Code requires that investigators pursue all reasonable lines of enquiry — whether they point toward or away from a suspect. The Task Log / Actions Register (Task 4.1) and the documented Negative Results (Task 4.2) are the primary evidence that this duty was discharged. If a line of enquiry was not pursued, the decision must appear in the Policy File (Task 4.3) with its documented rationale.
*   **Duty to Record and Retain:** All material obtained or generated in the course of the investigation must be recorded and retained, regardless of whether it is used in the prosecution. This encompasses the entire Evidence Register.
*   **Disclosure to the Defence:** The prosecution must disclose to the defence any material that might reasonably be considered capable of undermining the prosecution case or assisting the defence. Identifying and scheduling this material — not producing the MG5 itself — is the most legally consequential aspect of this task.

### 2. MG5 Structure and Content Requirements
The MG5 is a factual, objective, chronological summary of the evidence. It is not an analytical product — it does not contain intelligence assessments, probabilistic judgments, personal opinions, or investigative theory.
*   **Case Summary Narrative:** A chronological account of the offence and the investigation, written in factual language, covering: the nature of the alleged offence, the circumstances in which it came to the attention of investigators, the sequence of investigative actions taken, the evidence obtained and the means by which it was obtained, and the suspect's response (interviews, no comment, written statements).
*   **Points to Prove:** An explicit statement of the legal elements that must be established to prove the charged offence, and a reference to the specific evidence addressing each element. This links the evidence to the legal threshold, not to an analytical conclusion.
*   **Witness Summary:** A list of prosecution witnesses and the evidence each will give, including references to their statements.
*   **Exhibit Schedule:** A list of physical and digital exhibits with their unique reference numbers, descriptions, and storage locations. Every item in this schedule must appear in the Evidence Register with a valid hash value (if digital).
*   **Factual Language Requirement:** The MG5 must use factual, neutral language throughout. Expressions that attribute motive, character, or intent are prohibited. "The suspect entered the premises" is appropriate. "The suspect clearly intended to commit fraud" is not. The analyst must also strip out any reference to investigative methodology that has not been disclosed — intelligence tradecraft, covert source identities, and technical collection methods must not appear in the MG5.

### 3. Separation of Sensitive Material (MG6 Schedules)
The CPIA Code of Practice requires that sensitive material be separately scheduled rather than included in the main prosecution file.
*   **MG6C — Sensitive Material Schedule:** Material that the prosecution claims should not be disclosed to the defence on public interest immunity grounds. This includes material whose disclosure would identify a covert human intelligence source, reveal surveillance techniques, or compromise ongoing operations. The schedule records the existence of the material and the basis for sensitivity without revealing the content to the defence.
*   **MG6D — Retained Material Not Sensitive:** Material that is retained in the investigation file but is not included in the prosecution file and is not claimed as sensitive. This schedules unused material that does not undermine the prosecution or assist the defence but must nonetheless be recorded to demonstrate disclosure compliance.
*   **Compartmentalisation Principle:** The MG5 and MG6 schedules are distinct documents with distinct disclosure paths. Material that cannot be disclosed without endangering sources or operations must be recorded on an MG6 schedule, not omitted from the disclosure record entirely. Omission is not an acceptable alternative to protected scheduling.

### 4. Interface with the Investigation's Analytical Products
The MG5 does not draw on the Analytical Briefing (Step 13) — the two products serve different audiences and have fundamentally different purposes. The MG5 draws directly from the investigation's primary records.
*   **Evidence Register:** The source of every exhibit listed in the MG5. Hash values and archive records from Task 5.1 underpin the chain of custody for digital evidence.
*   **Task Log / Actions Register:** The source of evidence that all reasonable lines of enquiry were pursued (including negative results). This is the primary defence against a CPIA disclosure challenge.
*   **Source Register:** Informs what sources are sensitive and must be moved to MG6C rather than appearing in the MG5.
*   **Chronological Matrix:** The Phase 4 master chronology (Task 9.1) is the structural backbone of the MG5 narrative — it provides the UTC-normalised timeline of events from which the factual narrative is constructed.

### 5. Quality Standards
*   **No Opinion or Analysis:** The MG5 is stripped of analytical language. Every sentence must be a verifiable factual statement attributable to a specific source. Where a fact is contested, both versions are recorded with their respective sources.
*   **No Covert Tactics Disclosure:** Any reference to investigative methods (surveillance, covert data collection, undercover operations, technical interception) that is not already known to the defence must be removed from the MG5 and placed on the appropriate MG6 schedule.
*   **CPIA Compliance Audit:** Before the MG5 is submitted, a formal review is conducted to confirm that all material in the Evidence Register has been scheduled (either in the prosecution file or on the appropriate MG6 form), that no sensitive material appears in the MG5, and that the negative results documented in Task 4.2 are referenced in the MG5 narrative or accompanying unused material schedules.
*   **Legal Advice Coordination:** Where the investigation's findings involve complex financial structures, offshore jurisdictions, or digital evidence at the frontier of legal admissibility, the MG5 preparation should be conducted in consultation with the CPS reviewing lawyer to ensure the evidential presentation meets prosecutorial requirements before submission.
