Task 1.3, the **Legal and Ethical Justification (PLAN Framework)**, is the critical evaluative filter that must be applied before any investigative vector is authorized. While Task 1.2 (STEEPLES) identifies the external factors of the environment, Task 1.3 uses the **PLAN** framework to assess the proposed investigative choices against a rigorous set of legal and ethical standards. This ensures that every action is defensible, human-rights compliant, and targeted specifically at the knowledge gaps identified in Task 1.1.

The following is an exhaustive breakdown of the subtasks and procedural requirements for Task 1.3:

### 1. Proportionality Assessment (The Intrusion Balance)
The investigator must evaluate whether the proposed method of collection is "proportionate" to the operational goal and the seriousness of the matter.
*   **1.3.1: Balancing Intrusion vs. Objective.** The investigator must explicitly weigh the level of intrusion into a subject's privacy against the severity of the incident or the value of the intelligence requirement. 
*   **1.3.2: Collateral Intrusion Review.** A subtask is to identify if the investigative vector will capture data on "non-subjects" (collateral intrusion) and whether this unintended impact is justified by the mission's goal.
*   **1.3.3: Scaling the Response.** The assessment must confirm that the investigator is not using "sledgehammer" methods for "nut" problems—for instance, ensuring that a full-scale corporate audit is not triggered for a minor administrative discrepancy unless the goal specifically demands it.

### 2. Legality Verification (The Statutory Basis)
Every investigative action must have a clear and articulable legal basis to ensure the resulting evidence is admissible and the investigator is protected from liability.
*   **1.3.4: Identifying the Legal Mandate.** The investigator must cite the specific statutory or regulatory authority allowing the collection (e.g., GDPR Article 6(1)(f) for "Legitimate Interest," the UK’s Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act (CPIA), or the Berkeley Protocol for human rights investigations).
*   **1.3.5: Jurisdictional Compliance.** If the investigation crosses borders, the investigator must verify that the method is legal in *both* the jurisdiction of the investigator and the jurisdiction where the data resides.
*   **1.3.6: Admissibility Check.** The investigator must evaluate whether the current "legal path" will lead to material that can be disclosed in court or to a regulatory body, adhering to standards like the UK MG5 for objective reporting.

### 3. Accountability and Audit Readiness
Accountability ensures that every decision is owned by an identifiable individual and is recorded in a way that withstands future scrutiny.
*   **1.3.7: Decision Ownership.** The **Case Decision Log (CDL)** must explicitly record the identity of the person making the decision and the specific information they had available at that exact moment.
*   **1.3.8: Contemporaneous Documentation.** Investigators are mandated to record the "Assessment of Choices" in real-time, preventing "hindsight bias" where an investigator tries to justify a failed decision using information that only became known later.
*   **1.3.9: Transparency of Methodology.** The process must be documented so it is "reproducible"—meaning an independent reviewer could follow the logic and arrive at the same conclusion regarding the necessity of the vector.

### 4. Necessity and Alternative Evaluation
Necessity dictates that a specific investigative action should only be taken if there is no less-intrusive way to fill the identified knowledge gap.
*   **1.3.10: Gap-Action Linkage.** The investigator must prove that the chosen action is directly required to answer an **Intelligence Requirement (IR)** defined in Step 2. 
*   **1.3.11: Evaluation of Less Intrusive Alternatives.** As a mandatory subtask, the investigator must document why other, less intrusive methods were rejected. For example, if "Tier 1" official records (government registers) could answer the question, a "Tier 3" social media deep-dive may be deemed unnecessary and thus unethical.
*   **1.3.12: The Inconsistency Principle Preview.** Even at this early stage, the investigator should assess whether the proposed necessity is based on "confirmation bias"—seeking only the data that proves a theory—or if it is designed to test a variety of alternative hypotheses.

### Integration into the Workflow
The final output of Task 1.3 is an **"Assessment of Choices" matrix** within the Case Decision Log. This matrix lists each "Preferred Option" (investigative vector) alongside its PLAN justification. If a vector cannot satisfy all four pillars of the PLAN framework, it must be recorded as a **"Negative Decision"** in Task 1.4, documenting exactly why that line of enquiry was abandoned on legal or ethical grounds.
