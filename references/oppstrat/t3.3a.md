Task 3.3, the **Supervisory Review**, serves as the primary "quality gate" and accountability checkpoint within the investigative cycle. In professional environments—particularly those adhering to the UK College of Policing’s Authorised Professional Practice (APP) or the National Intelligence Model (NIM)—this review is a mandatory procedural requirement designed to certify that the investigation is both strategically sound and legally defensible before significant resources are expended.

The following is a detailed breakdown of the components, standards, and objectives of the Supervisory Review:

### 1. Temporal Constraints and Procedural Mandate
The review is doctrinally required to take place within a **24–96 hour window** following the initiation of the investigation plan.
*   **Priority Alignment:** For serious or priority offences, the initial plan must typically be drafted by the end of the first operational day. The supervisor then has a strictly defined period to review this material, ensuring that the "Direction" phase of the intelligence cycle was handled correctly.
*   **Contemporaneous Audit:** The review is intended to capture the investigator's state of mind at the outset, preventing the corruption of the audit trail through retroactive logging or hindsight bias.

### 2. Certification of the Analytical Infrastructure
The supervisor must scrutinize the documents produced in Phase 1—specifically the **Case Decision & Collection Log (CDL)** and the **Investigation Plan / Strategy Document**.

*   **Validation of the STEEPLES Assessment:** The supervisor reviews the environmental factors identified (Social, Technical, Economic, Environmental, Political, Legal, Ethical, and Safety). They must certify that the investigator has accurately mapped the "influencing factors" of the operational environment and identified potential risks to safety or operational security (OPSEC).
*   **Verification of the PLAN Framework:** This is the most critical legal checkpoint. The supervisor must formally certify that the proposed investigative vectors are:
    *   **Proportionate:** The level of intrusion is balanced against the gravity of the goal.
    *   **Legal:** There is a clear statutory or ethical mandate (such as CPIA or GDPR compliance).
    *   **Accountable:** Decisions are clearly attributed to a specific officer or analyst.
    *   **Necessary:** No less-intrusive means exist to fill the identified knowledge gaps.

### 3. Strategy and Resource Soundness
Beyond legal compliance, the supervisor assesses the practical viability of the strategy.
*   **Intelligence Requirements (IRs):** The reviewer checks that the IRs are specific, answerable, and directly linked to the operational goals. They ensure the investigation is not collecting "orphan data" that serves no diagnostic purpose.
*   **Line of Enquiry Prioritisation:** The supervisor verifies that the "diagnostic" leads (those most likely to disprove alternative hypotheses) have been ranked as high priority.
*   **Resource Allocation:** They confirm that tasks are correctly assigned to investigators or automated tools and that deadlines are realistic and adhere to timeliness standards.

### 4. Mitigation of Failure Modes
The Supervisory Review is specifically designed to catch common "investigative traps" early in the process:
*   **Preventing Scope Creep:** The supervisor ensures the investigation has not already expanded beyond its original Terms of Reference (ToR) without re-authorisation.
*   **Neutralising Confirmation Bias:** The reviewer looks for evidence that the investigator is seeking disconfirming data, as mandated by the Inconsistency Principle. They may require the investigator to add "Analysis of Competing Hypotheses" (ACH) markers even at this early stage.
*   **Audit Trail Integrity:** The supervisor verifies that the CDL is being maintained in real-time and that all "negative decisions" (leads not pursued) are documented with a valid rationale.

### 5. Final Authorisation and the Case Decision Log
The result of Task 3.3 is an immutable entry in the **Case Decision Log (CDL)**.
*   **Supervisory Signature:** The supervisor records their identity, the date/time of the review, and their decision to either certify the plan or mandate specific amendments.
*   **Review Triggers:** The supervisor establishes future "Review Triggers"—specific events or timeframes (e.g., every 30 days) that will require the investigation to return for further high-level oversight.

By conducting this review within the 24–96 hour window, professional organisations ensure that the investigation is "right at the start," providing a defensive bulwark that protects the integrity of the findings through to the final summary and dissemination phases.
