Task 3.1, **Case Summary Record Initiation**, is the administrative opening of an investigation. It moves the process from a conceptual idea into a formal, trackable project. This task utilizes the **Case Summary Record Template**, which is categorized as a "General Case Overview" tool designed for rapid consumption by decision-makers while maintaining objective distance.

The initiation of this record involves three primary administrative pillars:

### 1. Administrative Identifiers
The first step is establishing the metadata that will identify the case across all databases and registers. This ensures that every subsequent piece of evidence or task can be linked back to a single source of truth.
*   **Case Reference Number:** A unique, sequential identifier used to index the investigation within the system.
*   **Title:** A concise, descriptive name for the operation (e.g., "Operation Obsidian").
*   **Classification:** Defining the sensitivity level (e.g., Restricted, Confidential) to dictate who can access the record in the **Entity Register** or **Dissemination Log**.

### 2. Operational Parameters
Beyond simple naming, Task 3.1 requires defining the "who" and "when" of the inquiry to ensure accountability.
*   **Investigation Type and Purpose:** A brief, one-paragraph summary explaining why the investigation is being conducted (e.g., corporate due diligence, asset recovery, or criminal intelligence).
*   **Lead Investigator and Team Composition:** Explicitly naming the individuals responsible for the case, which is a core requirement for "Accountability" within the **PLAN** framework.
*   **Temporal Markers:** Recording the date the case was opened, its current status, and the target completion date.

### 3. The Initial Scope Statement
The most critical analytical component of Task 3.1 is the **Scope Statement**, often referred to as the **Terms of Reference (ToR)**. This is not a summary of findings, but a defensive boundary set before the investigation begins.

*   **Defining Boundaries:** The statement must explicitly define what is *in scope* and what is *out of scope*. This includes specifying the geographic jurisdictions to be searched, the types of entities involved, and the specific questions (Intelligence Requirements) the investigation seeks to answer.
*   **Preventing "Scope Creep":** A well-defined scope statement serves as a safeguard against the unauthorized expansion of the investigation. The methodology dictates that any expansion beyond these initial boundaries requires a formal "Policy Deviation" entry in the **Case Decision Log** and a documented re-authorization.
*   **Legal and Ethical Framing:** The scope must align with the **PLAN** framework (Proportionality, Legality, Accountability, Necessity), ensuring the investigation remains a "reasonable line of enquiry" that can withstand legal or regulatory disclosure.

In the broader workflow, this record acts as the "cover sheet" for the entire case file. While the **Investigation Plan** (Task 2.1) handles the gritty details of *how* to collect data, the **Case Summary Record** provides the high-level justification for *why* the investigation exists and what its legal limits are.
