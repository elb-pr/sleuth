Task 3.2, **Defining Terms of Reference (ToR)**, is a critical administrative and legal safeguard within the **Direction** phase of the intelligence cycle. Its primary objective is to establish a "defensive boundary" for the inquiry, explicitly stating the legal or ethical mandate to prevent "scope creep"—the unauthorized and often unlawful expansion of an investigation beyond its original purpose. This task ensures that the investigation remains proportionate, necessary, and auditable from the outset.

The following is an excruciatingly detailed breakdown of the components and subtasks involved in establishing the Terms of Reference:

### 1. Establishing the Legal or Ethical Mandate
Before any data is collected, the investigator must define the specific authority under which they are operating. This is the foundation of the **Legality** and **Accountability** pillars of the PLAN framework.
*   **1.1: Identifying Statutory Authority:** For law enforcement, this involves citing specific legislation such as the **Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act (CPIA)** or the **UK College of Policing’s Authorised Professional Practice (APP)**.
*   **1.2: Defining Ethical Frameworks:** In non-law enforcement contexts, such as human rights or journalistic investigations, the mandate is often derived from the **Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations**, which emphasizes principles of dignity, responsibility, and proportionality.
*   **1.3: Lawful Basis for Processing:** Under privacy laws like **GDPR**, the ToR must document the "lawful basis" for collecting personal data, such as "legitimate interest," ensuring that the investigation does not violate data protection standards.

### 2. Defining Scope Boundaries (Spatial, Temporal, and Entity)
The ToR must provide "scope boundaries" to ensure the investigation remains focused on the primary intelligence requirements.
*   **2.1: Temporal Boundaries:** Explicitly stating the time period under investigation (e.g., "financial transactions between 2022 and 2024"). This prevents the unnecessary collection of historical data that is no longer relevant.
*   **2.2: Geospatial Boundaries:** Defining the geographic areas or specific jurisdictions (e.g., "corporate registries in the UK and BVI") where enquiries will be conducted.
*   **2.3: Entity Limitations:** Identifying the specific individuals, organizations, or assets that are the subject of the inquiry. This limits the investigation to "diagnostic" entities and prevents the collection of "orphan records" with no analytical value.

### 3. Purpose Limitation and Data Minimization
A central subtask of the ToR is the implementation of **Purpose Limitation**, ensuring that data collection is strictly limited to information relevant to the defined investigation objective.
*   **3.1: Defining the "Points to Prove":** The ToR must outline the specific legal or strategic elements required to satisfy the goal (e.g., "proving beneficial ownership"). This ensures every action is a "reasonable line of enquiry".
*   **3.2: Minimizing Collateral Intrusion:** The investigator must assess and document how they will avoid collecting data on "non-subjects" or entering into areas of private life unrelated to the investigation.

### 4. Mitigation of Scope Creep
The ToR acts as a "contract" for the investigation. Any deviation from this document is flagged as a failure mode that requires formal mitigation.
*   **4.1: Mandatory Review Cycles:** The methodology requires that the ToR be reviewed regularly against the **Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP)** under supervisory oversight to ensure the investigation has not drifted.
*   **4.2: Policy Deviation Protocol:** If a new lead emerges that falls outside the original scope, the investigator is forbidden from pursuing it until a formal "Policy Deviation" is recorded in the **Case Decision Log (CDL)**, providing a new rationale and seeking re-authorization.
*   **4.3: Defining Closure Criteria:** The ToR must state the conditions under which the investigation will be considered complete, preventing it from continuing indefinitely without a clear objective.

### 5. Integration with the Case Summary Record
Finally, the ToR is synthesized into the **Case Summary Record**, which provides the high-level administrative overview of the investigation for stakeholders. This record includes the case reference, title, and a concise summary of the purpose, serving as the "master document" that governs all subsequent activity in the **Task Log** and **Evidence Register**.
