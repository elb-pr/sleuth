Task 3.4a, **Collection Authorisation and Phase Transition Gate**, is the formal checkpoint that closes the Direction phase and authorises the transition into active Collection. No investigative action beyond desk-based planning may proceed until this gate is passed. The task exists to ensure that every prerequisite from Phase 1 has been satisfied, that the analytical infrastructure is correctly configured, and that a clear, auditable record exists of the moment the investigation moved from strategic planning to operational execution.

The detailed procedural breakdown of this task is as follows:

### 1. Pre-Collection Readiness Checklist
Before authorisation is granted, the investigator must confirm that every foundational document produced in Phase 1 meets the required standard. This is not a cursory review — it is a systematic audit against a fixed checklist.

*   **Case Decision Log (CDL) Completeness:** Verify that the CDL contains: the initial situation and circumstances record (Task 1.1), all operational goals with defined success criteria, a complete gap analysis producing specific Intelligence Requirements, and a fully documented STEEPLES environmental assessment. Every negative decision made during the Direction phase must already be logged.
*   **Investigation Strategy Document:** Confirm that the strategy document (Task 2.1 onwards) contains: prioritised lines of enquiry ranked by diagnostic value, a tiered source hierarchy for both open and closed collection, resource allocation with named responsibilities, and realistic deadlines aligned with the case timeline.
*   **Terms of Reference (ToR):** Verify that the ToR (Task 3.2a) explicitly defines: temporal boundaries, geospatial boundaries, entity limitations, the lawful basis for data processing, and the policy deviation protocol for scope expansion.
*   **PLAN Framework Certification:** Confirm that the supervisory review (Task 3.3a) has formally certified every proposed investigative vector against Proportionality, Legality, Accountability, and Necessity criteria. Vectors that failed certification must be recorded as negative decisions with rationale.
*   **Case Summary Record:** Verify that the administrative record (Task 3.1a) is complete with case reference number, classification level, lead investigator, team composition, and the initial scope statement.

### 2. Toolkit and Infrastructure Commissioning
The transition gate requires that the technical infrastructure for collection and evidence preservation is operational before any data enters the workspace.

*   **Evidence Preservation Pipeline:** Confirm that the SHA-256 hashing and WARC archiving pipeline (`evidence_preservation.py`) is tested and functional. Run a test capture against a benign URL to verify hash computation, Wayback Machine submission, and custody manifest generation.
*   **Entity Database Initialisation:** Verify that the Entity Register (`assets/entity-database/entities.json`) is initialised with the correct schema and that `database_manager.py check` returns a clean integrity report. If the investigation involves geospatial elements, confirm the geo-database schema is also initialised.
*   **Source Grading System:** Confirm familiarity with the Admiralty 6x6 Matrix by running `source_grader.py matrix` and reviewing the grading criteria. Establish the default reliability grade for each planned source category (e.g., A for government registries, F for anonymous tips).
*   **Content Archiver Availability:** If the investigation will involve social media or multimedia evidence, verify that the required external tools (yt-dlp, gallery-dl, Playwright) are installed and functional via `content_archiver.py`.

### 3. Operational Security (OPSEC) Final Assessment
Before any external-facing collection begins, the investigator must formally assess and document the operational security posture.

*   **Digital Footprint Minimisation:** Document the measures in place to prevent the investigation's collection activities from being detected by the subject or hostile third parties. This includes: browser profile isolation, VPN or proxy configuration, rate limiting for automated queries, and account separation for social media research.
*   **Counter-Detection Awareness:** Assess whether the subject or associated entities are likely to be monitoring for investigative activity (e.g., through domain lookup notifications, social media analytics, or corporate intelligence services). If counter-detection risk is assessed as medium or above, document specific mitigation measures in the CDL.
*   **Data Handling Protocols:** Confirm that captured data will be stored in accordance with the classification level defined in the Case Summary Record. Document the encryption standards, access controls, and backup procedures for the investigation's data holdings.

### 4. Formal Authorisation Record
The transition gate concludes with a formal authorisation entry in the Case Decision Log.

*   **Authorisation Statement:** A dated, signed statement confirming that: all Phase 1 prerequisites have been satisfied, the analytical infrastructure is operational, the OPSEC assessment is complete, and the investigation is authorised to proceed to active collection under the defined Terms of Reference.
*   **Constraints and Conditions:** Any conditions or limitations attached to the authorisation must be explicitly recorded. For example: "Collection authorised for open sources only; closed source collection requires separate authorisation under [statute/policy]."
*   **Review Schedule:** The authorisation must specify the next mandatory review point — typically aligned with the first supervisory review cycle defined in Task 3.3a (24-96 hours from first collection activity).
*   **Point of No Return Documentation:** Once this gate is passed, any subsequent change to the Terms of Reference, operational goals, or scope boundaries constitutes a Policy Deviation and must follow the formal deviation protocol (Task 3.2a, Section 4.2). This constraint must be acknowledged in the authorisation record.

### 5. Phase Transition Handoff
The final subtask is the formal handoff from the Direction phase to the Collection phase.

*   **Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP) Activation:** The ICP produced during gap analysis (Task 1.1) is formally activated. All Intelligence Requirements are now "live" and collection activities may begin against the prioritised source list.
*   **Task Log Initialisation:** The Task Log (`templates/database/task-log.md`) is opened with its first entry recording the authorisation and the commencement of active collection. Every subsequent investigative action, including negative results, will be logged here.
*   **Evidence Register Initialisation:** The Evidence Register (`templates/database/evidence-register.md`) is opened and ready to receive the first captured artifacts. The register must be pre-configured with the SHA-256 hashing protocol and the custody chain fields defined in Task 5.1.
