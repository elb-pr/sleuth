Task 2.2, the **Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP)**, is the operational blueprint that dictates how, where, and when the information required to answer the Intelligence Requirements (IRs) will be gathered. It serves as a dynamic control document that ensures the collection phase remains proportionate, targeted, and methodologically sound, preventing the common failure mode of "single-source reliance."

### 1. Implementation of the Parallel Sourcing Strategy
The ICP must mandate a "parallel sourcing" approach to ensure that findings are not based on a single point of failure or potential disinformation.

*   **Task 2.2.1: Cross-Platform Mapping.** The investigator identifies both open sources (OSINT) and closed/proprietary sources (financial records, internal databases) that can answer a single IR.
*   **Task 2.2.2: Redundancy Planning.** The plan requires at least two independent source types for every high-priority finding to satisfy the "three-source rule" for corroboration.
*   **Task 2.2.3: Source Diversity Check.** An explicit subtask is to ensure that the source base includes different *types* of evidence (e.g., geospatial imagery paired with corporate filings) to prevent "echo chamber" reporting where multiple sources are actually derived from the same underlying data.

### 2. Defining the Hierarchical Sequence of Collection
To maintain professional rigor and cost-effectiveness, the ICP establishes a tiered hierarchy for collection, dictating the order in which sources are queried.

*   **Task 2.2.4: Tier 1 (Official/Statutory) Prioritization.** The collection begins with official government records, such as electoral registers, land registries, and statutory company filings (e.g., Companies House or SEC EDGAR).
*   **Task 2.2.5: Tier 2 (Verified Commercial) Integration.** Once the official baseline is established, investigators move to regulated commercial databases and financial services like Bloomberg, Orbis, or credit reference agencies.
*   **Task 2.2.6: Tier 3 & 4 (Social/Secondary) Enrichment.** The sequence concludes with more volatile sources, including social media, self-published content, and secondary news commentary, which are used to fill gaps in the official record.
*   **Task 2.2.7: Sequence Justification.** For each IR, the investigator must document why a lower-tier source is being used if a higher-tier source was available, ensuring the "Necessity" pillar of the PLAN framework is upheld.

### 3. Digital Chain of Custody and "Evidence Vault" Standards
The ICP must establish the technical protocols for preserving material to an evidentiary standard suitable for legal or regulatory disclosure.

*   **Task 2.2.8: Evidence Register Configuration.** The investigator initializes the **Evidence / Material Register**, which will track every item’s reference, description, source, and storage location.
*   **Task 2.2.9: Automated URL Archiving.** The plan mandates the use of specialized browser extensions like **Hunchly** to automatically track every URL visited, timestamp page captures, and record IP footprints in real-time.
*   **Task 2.2.10: Cryptographic Hashing Protocol.** A mandatory subtask is the application of cryptographic hashing (such as SHA-256) to every digital file or screenshot at the exact moment of collection.
*   **Task 2.2.11: Immutable Storage (The Evidence Vault).** The ICP defines the "Evidence Vault" requirements, ensuring that all collected material is moved into read-only, access-logged storage to prevent accidental or malicious alteration.

### 4. Operational Logistics and Resource Allocation
The final component of the ICP is the practical management of the investigative team and timeline.

*   **Task 2.2.12: Resource and Personnel Tasking.** Specific collection tasks are assigned to individual researchers or automated agents within the **Task Log / Actions Register**.
*   **Task 2.2.13: Defining Collection Deadlines.** Every line of enquiry is assigned a deadline to ensure the investigation remains timely (a core ICD 203 standard).
*   **Task 2.2.14: Source Contact and Handling Logs.** For closed or human sources, the ICP maintains a **Source Register** that records reliability ratings (Admiralty grades), access methods, and any handling restrictions or legal protections required.
*   **Task 2.2.15: Review and Escalation Triggers.** The plan defines "Review Triggers"—specific findings or obstacles that mandate a pause in collection for a supervisory review of the original strategy.
