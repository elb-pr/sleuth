Task 1.1, **Situation and Circumstance Scoping**, is the foundational element of the **Direction** phase of the Intelligence Cycle. This task is executed through the **Case Decision & Collection Log (CDL) Template**, which is designed to ensure that the investigation is conducted with professional rigour, objective reasoning, and auditable logic from its inception. By recording the "contemporaneous state of mind" of the investigator, this task prevents hindsight bias—the tendency to judge past decisions based on information that only became available later.

The excruciating breakdown of this task involves the following structured layers:

### 1. Recording the Current Known Situation
Before any active collection begins, the investigator must immutably record the baseline of the inquiry within the **Case Decision Log (CDL)**.
*   **Documentation of Circumstances:** The investigator must detail the "Situation and Circumstances" as they are currently understood. This involves listing confirmed facts, the origin of the initial lead, and any existing evidence already in the "Evidence Vault".
*   **Contemporaneous Entry:** These entries must be made in real-time. In a professional or legal setting, if digital systems are unavailable, this requires the use of sequentially numbered, bound paper books where blank spaces are struck through to prevent retroactive alteration.
*   **Preventing Workspace Poisoning:** By documenting the situation at the start, the investigator establishes a clean "epistemic gate," ensuring that subsequent data can be graded for its ability to corroborate or contradict this initial baseline.

### 2. Defining Operational Goals (Articulating Success)
The second component of Task 1.1 is defining the "Aims and Outcome" of the investigation.
*   **Strategic Parameters:** Success must be defined by clear, high-level objectives—often called "Strategic Intelligence Requirements". For instance, a goal might be to "identify the ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) of Entity X" or "map the kinship-based control structures of an organization".
*   **Defining Success:** Success is articulated by describing what the final "surviving hypothesis" would look like once it has passed through the **Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH)** matrix.
*   **Scope Limitation (Terms of Reference):** Clearly articulating success serves to define the "Terms of Reference" (ToR) for the investigation. This is a critical defensive measure to prevent **"Scope Creep"**—the unauthorized expansion of an investigation beyond its original legal or ethical mandate.

### 3. Gap Analysis (Identifying Knowledge Gaps)
Once the goals are set, the investigator must perform a "Gap Analysis" to identify the specific information missing that prevents those goals from being achieved.
*   **Intelligence Requirements (IRs):** The broad operational goals are broken down into specific, answerable questions known as Intelligence Requirements. These IRs act as the bridge between "what we know" and "what we need to find out".
*   **Prioritizing Gaps:** Not all gaps are equal. The investigator must rank these knowledge gaps into "Priority Lines of Enquiry". This ensures that resources are directed toward the most "diagnostic" evidence first—evidence that has the highest potential to disprove alternative hypotheses.
*   **Output: The Intelligence Collection Plan (ICP):** The final output of the Gap Analysis is the **Intelligence Collection Plan**. This dynamic document details the specific information required, deadlines for collection, and the prioritized sources (both open and closed) that will be used to fill the identified gaps.

### 4. Framing through Influencing Factors (STEEPLES & PLAN)
Task 1.1 requires the investigator to justify the scoping of the investigation through two primary analytical frameworks:
*   **STEEPLES Framework:** The investigator must assess the environmental factors influencing the case: **S**ocial, **T**echnical, **E**conomic, **E**nvironmental, **P**olitical, **L**egal, **E**thical, and **S**afety. This provides the "Contextual Intelligence" required to understand the operational environment.
*   **PLAN Framework:** All proposed investigative vectors to fill the identified gaps must be evaluated for **P**roportionality, **L**egality, **A**ccountability, and **N**ecessity. This ensures that the investigation is conducted within legal boundaries, such as the UK's **CPIA** or **GDPR**.

### 5. Mandatory Recording of "Negative Decisions"
A vital part of scoping is deciding what *not* to investigate. The CDL mandates that **"Negative Decisions"**—conscious choices not to pursue a specific line of inquiry—must be recorded.
*   **Auditability:** Recording why a lead was abandoned or why a specific goal was deemed out of scope is essential for demonstrating that "all reasonable lines of enquiry" were pursued.
*   **Disclosure Compliance:** In law enforcement contexts, such as the **UK MG5 standard**, these logs are critical for legal disclosure, as they prove the investigator acted without narrative bias by considering and documenting the rejection of alternative paths.
