# Investigation Strategy & Intelligence Requirement Plan

1. Administrative Context & Case Foundations

The administrative foundation of an investigation is not merely a clerical prerequisite; it constitutes the legal and ethical anchor for all operational activity. By establishing these parameters, the Intelligence Architect ensures a robust framework for accountability and prevents "scope creep"—the undisciplined expansion of research that dilutes resources and risks privacy violations. These foundations provide the mandate for data processing and ensure the investigation remains defensible under the scrutiny of legal and regulatory bodies.

Mission Statement & Reasonable Lines of Enquiry

Command: The investigation team is mandated to pursue all "Reasonable Lines of Enquiry," as defined by the UK College of Policing Authorised Professional Practice (APP). 2. Intelligence Requirements (IR) & Essential Elements of Information (EEI)

Intelligence Requirements (IRs) are the strategic pillars of the inquiry. They transform vague curiosity into a structured "Intelligence Cycle" consisting of Direction, Collection, Processing, Analysis, and Dissemination. By defining specific IRs, the strategist ensures that collection is purposeful and yields a product capable of supporting high-stakes decision-making.

Priority Intelligence Requirements

1. [Core Question] (e.g., "Who is the Ultimate Beneficial Owner of Entity X?")
  * The "So What?" Layer: Determining the UBO is critical to identifying potential sanctions evasion, undisclosed conflicts of interest, or hidden control structures that could jeopardize legal compliance or reputational standing.
  * Essential Elements of Information (EEIs): Full legal name, Date of Birth (DOB), residential addresses, shareholding percentages, identification document numbers, and relationship to intermediate holding vehicles.
2. [Core Question] (e.g., "What is the historical land-use evolution of Location Y?")
  * The "So What?" Layer: Understanding physical site changes reveals concealed infrastructure, undeclared operational capacity, or coordination between entities that align with the timeline of suspected illicit activity.
  * Essential Elements of Information (EEIs): Historical satellite imagery, planning applications, land registry ownership records, georeferenced historical maps, and building control records.

3. Entity Schema & Data Architecture (POLE Model)

To maintain interoperability and prevent "Schema Rigidity" in long-term inquiries, the investigation mandates a standardized entity schema based on the POLE (Person, Object, Location, Event) model. This ensures data is optimized for link analysis and cross-system integration.

Master Entity Schema Guide

Entity Type	Minimum Required Fields	Standard Metadata Fields
Person	Full Name, DOB, Nationality, Gender	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling
Organization	Name, Registration Number, Country, Type	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling
Location	Address/Coordinates, Type (Res/Comm)	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling
Account	Account Type, Identifier, Platform	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling
Vehicle	VIN, Registration Plate, Make, Model	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling

Document	Type, Title, Date, Hash Value	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling
Event	Event Type, Date/Time, Location	Source Attribution, Confidence Level, Last Verified, Analyst Identity, Classification/Handling

Entity Resolution Logic

Command: Apply rigorous Entity Resolution to determine if disparate records refer to the same real-world entity. Records sharing two or more independent identifiers (e.g., Name + DOB or Email + Address) may be merged.

Command: Define Typed Links. All relationships between entities must be recorded as "Typed Links" with directional properties and metadata. Standard links include: ASSOCIATED_WITH, OWNS, EMPLOYS, COMMUNICATES_WITH, and DIRECTOR_OF. Every link must carry a date range, a strength indicator (High/Moderate/Low), and full source attribution.

Standardizing entity definitions allows for the strategic sourcing of evidence from a hierarchical perspective.

4. Collection Strategy & Source Hierarchy

The selection of sources is a strategic decision that determines the weight of the findings. Reliability is assessed by contrasting Tier 1 (Official Government Records) against Tier 5 (Leaked Information), ensuring analysts do not over-value unverified data.

Source Evaluation Framework (NATO Admiralty 6x6)

Source Reliability (A-F)	Information Credibility (1-6)
A: Completely Reliable	1: Confirmed by other sources
B: Usually Reliable	2: Probably True
C: Fairly Reliable	3: Possibly True
D: Not Usually Reliable	4: Doubtful
E: Unreliable	5: Improbable
F: Reliability cannot be judged	6: Truth cannot be judged

Note: In OSINT, a rating of F6 (untested source/unverifiable info) is the OSINT Default. It is the starting point for analysis, not a reason for dismissal, requiring subsequent corroboration.

5. Analytical Methodology & Priority Lines of Enquiry (LOE)

7. Governance, Ethics, & Risk Management

In accordance with the Berkeley Protocol, investigative success is secondary to the principles of Safety, Dignity, Proportionality, Responsibility, and Transparency.

Risk & Failure Mode Mitigation

Failure Mode	Mitigation Strategy
Identity Confusion	Require minimum of two independent identifiers (e.g., DOB + Address) for resolution.
Stale Records	Mandatory "Date Observed" fields and periodic currency reviews every 30 days.
Source Conflation	Mandate source attribution at the field level, not record level; never merge source citations.
Circular Reporting	Trace all claims to primary sources; identify if multiple sources share one origin.
OPSEC Failures	Use of passive observation only; separate browser profiles and VPN protocols.

Legal & Ethical Checkpoints

* GDPR Compliance: Verify "Legitimate Interest" and "Lawful Basis" for processing personal data.
* Purpose Limitation: Ensure collection does not exceed the original investigative mandate.
* Data Minimization: Delete records found to have no analytical relevance to the objective.

Conclusion: This document is a "living" strategy. To maintain operational integrity and compliance with College of Policing standards, this plan and its associated logs must undergo formal review every 30 days.
