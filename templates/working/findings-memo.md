The Findings Memo is a specialized practitioner format designed to consolidate investigative results into a concise, objective overview for decision-makers. It serves as a final delivery mechanism within the general case overview category, ensuring that findings are communicated with professional rigour and auditable logic. This template architecture prioritizes the separation of raw data from analytical interpretation to prevent cognitive bias and maintain epistemic transparency. By structuring findings into confirmed data, unverified leads, and negative results, the memo demonstrates that all reasonable lines of enquiry have been pursued.

### Findings Memo Template

**ADMINISTRATIVE HEADER**
*   **Case Reference:** [Unique Case ID/Number].
*   **Subject/Target:** [Primary Entity or Operation Name].
*   **Date of Memo:** [Current Date in UTC].
*   **Lead Investigator:** [Analyst/Officer Identity].
*   **Classification:** [Handling restrictions and sensitivity level].

**1. BOTTOM LINE UP FRONT (BLUF)**
*   **Analytical Message:** Present the most significant findings and core judgements immediately at the start of the document.
*   **Core Judgement:** A one-paragraph summary of the investigation’s primary conclusion, explicitly using standardized probability language.
*   **Confidence Level:** State the overall confidence in the assessment (High, Moderate, or Low) based on the quality of the underlying evidence base.

**2. SOURCING AND CREDIBILITY SUMMARY**
*   **Source Base Description:** Provide a holistic assessment of the quality, credibility, and gaps within the consulted source base.
*   **Admiralty Rating:** Apply the Admiralty 6x6 Matrix to rate the overall reliability of the sources (A-F) and the credibility of the information (1-6).
*   **Material Gaps:** Explicitly note any critical information gaps or inaccessible sources that drive the key judgements or limit the findings.

**3. CONFIRMED AND CORROBORATED FINDINGS**
*   **Factual Summary:** List facts that have been definitively established through statutory filings, official government records, or primary documentation.
*   **Corroborated Findings:** Document findings supported by at least two independent and reliable sources.
*   **Source Attribution:** Ensure every finding ends with a direct citation to the specific evidence or material register entry that supports it.

**4. UNVERIFIED LEADS**
*   **Potential Findings:** Record information that is reasonably logical but lacks independent confirmation or remains partially consistent with other reporting.
*   **Follow-up Actions:** Identify specific tasks or collection requirements needed to transition these leads into confirmed findings.
*   **Epistemic Warning:** Clearly label these items as unverified to prevent them from being treated as established facts by decision-makers.

**5. NEGATIVE RESULTS**
*   **Lines of Enquiry Pursued:** Document specific searches or investigations that yielded no results.
*   **Analytical Significance:** Record the conscious choice not to pursue specific leads to demonstrate a balanced and thorough investigation.
*   **Auditability:** Negative findings are essential for legal and public scrutiny to prove that the investigator did not overlook reasonable possibilities.

**6. SCOPE LIMITATIONS AND CONSTRAINTS**
*   **Investigation Boundaries:** Define the strategic and tactical parameters that limited the extent of the enquiry.
*   **Technical/Legal Constraints:** List any impediments such as privacy law restrictions, platform API changes, or language barriers that hindered data collection.
*   **Purpose Limitation:** Confirm that the investigation stayed within its original legal or ethical mandate.

**7. CONCLUSIONS AND ANALYTICAL ASSESSMENT**
*   **Assessment of Findings:** Provide an interpretation of what the established facts mean in the context of the investigative goals.
*   **Alternative Hypotheses:** Briefly address plausible alternative explanations for the findings and the evidence used to discount them.
*   **Standardized Likelihood:** Use the ICD 203 7-tier scale (e.g., "Very Likely", "Unlikely") to express the probability of the conclusions.

**8. SOURCE INDEX AND EVIDENCE REGISTER**
*   **Material Registry:** Provide a structured list of every URL, document, or artifact cited within the memo.
*   **Chain of Custody:** Include cryptographic hash values and archival timestamps for digital evidence to ensure its integrity and admissibility.
*   **Metadata:** Document the date observed, source attribution, and analyst notes for every record.
