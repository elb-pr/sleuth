### Account Attribution Report Template

This template is designed to provide a rigorous, auditable structure for attributing a social media account to a specific real-world identity. It adheres to the Berkeley Protocol principles of accuracy and transparency, ensuring that findings distinguish between established facts and analytical inferences.

---

#### 1. Executive Summary
*   **Target Account:** [Full Username and Platform Handle].
*   **Attributed Identity:** [Name of the real-world individual or organization].
*   **Attribution Confidence Level:** [Select one: Confirmed, Probable, Possible, or Uncertain].
*   **Key Evidence Summary:** A 1–2 paragraph distillation of the most diagnostic evidence (e.g., unique identifiers, cross-platform matches, or direct real-world corroboration).

#### 2. Account Profile
*   **Platform:** [e.g., X (Twitter), Facebook, Telegram].
*   **Unique Identifiers:** [User ID numbers, permanent URLs].
*   **Profile Metadata:**
    *   **Display Name:** [Current and historical names used].
    *   **Account Creation Date:** [Exact timestamp in UTC if available].
    *   **Bio/Description:** [Text currently in the profile].
*   **Account Metrics:** [Follower/following counts, total post count, and posting frequency].

#### 3. Attribution Evidence
This section provides the logical bridge between the digital persona and the real-world entity, utilizing the "three-source rule" for independent corroboration where possible.

*   **Cross-Platform Account Matching:** Evidence of identical or near-identical handles, profile pictures, or bios used across different social media platforms (e.g., LinkedIn, Instagram, and X).
*   **Technical Identifiers:** Patterns in email addresses (e.g., recovery email fragments), phone numbers, or domain registrations associated with the account.
*   **Distinctive Content & Language Patterns:** Analysis of unique writing styles, specific slang, frequently used emojis, or "content fingerprints" such as identical hashtag sequences.
*   **Network & Association Analysis:** Evidence of connections to known associates, family members, or professional colleagues of the target identity.
*   **Real-World Verification:** Direct links to official government records, professional licenses (e.g., GMC or SRA), or corporate registrations that confirm the identity's details.

#### 4. Content & Activity Analysis
*   **Thematic Focus:** Dominant topics, interests, or narratives discussed by the account.
*   **Geographic Focus:** Location-based evidence derived from geotags, visual cues in media, or regional language variations.
*   **Temporal Analysis:** Analysis of posting times to determine the operator's time zone or operational hours.

#### 5. Confidence Assessment & Epistemic Framework
The assessment must strictly separate the reliability of the sources from the likelihood of the final judgment.

*   **Source Reliability (Admiralty A-F):** [Rate the overall trustworthiness of the sources used].
*   **Information Credibility (Admiralty 1-6):** [Rate the level of corroboration for the specific attribution claims].
*   **Analytical Confidence (ICD 203 Scale):** [Select: High, Moderate, or Low].
*   **Likelihood Assessment:** [Select from the ICD 203 7-tier scale: e.g., "Very Likely" (80–95%) or "Almost Certain" (95–99%)].

#### 6. Caveats and Information Gaps
*   **Limitations:** Note any inaccessible sources, privacy settings that hindered collection, or platform-specific data restrictions.
*   **Alternative Hypotheses:** Document any other identities considered and the evidence used to discount them.

#### 7. Source Registry & Archival Log
*   **Evidence Table:** A structured list of every URL or artifact cited.
*   **Archival Status:** [Select: Wayback Machine, Hunchly, or Local WARC] to ensure a digital chain of custody.
