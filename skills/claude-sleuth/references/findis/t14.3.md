# t14.3 — Evidence Vault Archiving

**Tool:** `evidence_preservation.py`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST run final hash verification on EVERY digital asset in the Evidence Register before transfer:
   - Recalculate the SHA-256 hash of every file.
   - Compare against the hash recorded at original capture (t5.1).
   - Any discrepancy MUST trigger immediate quarantine and investigation. You are REQUIRED to document the resolution. Proceeding with a hash mismatch is FORBIDDEN.
   - YOU MUST compile a Final Hash Log: every evidence item, original hash, final hash, verification result (match/mismatch).

2. ALWAYS transfer verified assets to the evidence vault with the following enforced:
   - **Read-only access:** Files MUST NOT be modified, deleted, or overwritten after transfer (WORM enforcement).
   - **Access logging:** Every access — including read-only — MUST be automatically logged with accessor identity, UTC timestamp, and files accessed.
   - **Redundancy:** You are REQUIRED to maintain a minimum of two geographically separated copies.

3. It is CRITICAL that the vault contains the complete digital record with no omissions:
   - **Primary evidence:** All collected artefacts with original filenames and directory structure.
   - **Derived analytical products:** Entity Register export, Chronological Matrix, Network Analysis outputs, ACH Matrix, Analytical Briefing.
   - **Administrative records:** Case Decision Log, Task Log, Source Register, Evidence Register metadata, Dissemination Log, Case Summary Record.
   - **Final Hash Log** from step 1.

4. YOU MUST define and document post-archiving access governance BEFORE archiving is completed:
   - Who has authority to grant access.
   - Retention period (varies by jurisdiction and case type).
   - Disposal protocol when retention expires (cryptographic destruction standard for sensitive material).

5. You are REQUIRED to issue Chain of Custody Certification: the lead investigator or evidence custodian MUST certify that the chain of custody is unbroken from original capture (t5.1) through all analytical phases to final archival. An uncertified archive is a complete systematic failure.
