# t15.3 — Strategic and Tactical Assessments (NIM)

**Template:** `working/nim.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST select the appropriate NIM product type before drafting — proceeding without selecting a product type is FORBIDDEN:
   - **Strategic Assessment:** 6–12 month planning horizon. Audience: force command. Feeds the Control Strategy.
   - **Tactical Assessment:** Current period (weekly/monthly). Audience: tactical commanders and Tasking and Coordination Groups (TCGs). Drives immediate operational deployment.
   - **Target Profile:** Specific individual or OCG. Draws from Subject/Corporate Profiles (t8.1) contextualised within NIM.
   - **Problem Profile:** Specific crime type, series, or problem area.
   - **Subject Profile:** Specific individual for enforcement or safeguarding purposes.

2. YOU MUST follow the **Strategic Assessment structure**:
   - Strategic Threat Picture: synthesis of current and emerging threats, each REQUIRED to be assessed using ICD 203 probability language.
   - Pattern and Trend Analysis: YOU MUST draw long-run trends from the Chronological Matrix (t9.1–9.3), identifying methodology shifts, geographic hotspot migration, network composition changes.
   - Resource Implications: what the threat picture demands in investigative capacity, specialist capabilities, inter-agency coordination.
   - Recommendations for the Control Strategy: which crime types or IRs should be elevated or deprioritised.
   - Source Base Assessment (per t13.4): YOU MUST include a transparent statement of source base with identified gaps.

3. YOU MUST follow the **Tactical Assessment structure**:
   - Current Threat Summary (BLUF equivalent): most operationally significant intelligence from the current period.
   - Emerging Threats and Opportunities: new threats and specific enforcement opportunities (planned criminal events, subjects in fixed locations, time-sensitive network vulnerabilities).
   - Ongoing Operation Updates: active investigations status with intelligence updates.
   - Tasking Recommendations for the TCG: YOU MUST provide specific actionable recommendations with supporting intelligence and assessed likelihood of success.
   - Outstanding IRs: ALWAYS include active unaddressed IRs from the ICP (t2.1) to be prioritised in the current tactical cycle.

4. YOU MUST feed from the investigation's analytical products:
   - Network Architecture (t10.2): Hub and Bridge Node identification MUST inform Target Profile prioritisation and tactical disruption points.
   - Chronological Matrix (t9.1–9.3): temporal patterns MUST populate pattern/trend sections.
   - ACH conclusion (t12.3): MUST become the principal threat assessment judgment.

5. ALWAYS apply these quality standards:
   - Every tactical recommendation MUST trace to a specific piece of intelligence. Unsourced recommendations are FORBIDDEN.
   - Review cycle compliance: tactical assessments REQUIRE inspector-level review within College of Policing APP timeframes. You are REQUIRED to record reviewer identity and date.
   - NIM products are intelligence assessments and recommendations — NOT operational orders.
   - Covert source identities MUST NOT appear in NIM products shared beyond the immediate intelligence team.
