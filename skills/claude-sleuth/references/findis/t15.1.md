# t15.1 — Issuing the Findings Memo

**Template:** `working/findings-memo.md`

## Procedure

1. It is CRITICAL that you understand: the Findings Memo is NOT a condensed Analytical Briefing. It is a structurally distinct operational product for field investigators, lead analysts, and case officers who need to act on the investigation's outputs. YOU MUST prioritise actionable precision over analytical narrative.

2. YOU MUST follow this fixed structure — omitting any section is FORBIDDEN:

   **Header:** Case reference number, investigation title, issuing analyst, date of issue, handling classification.

   **Confirmed Findings:** Numbered list. Each entry:
   - MUST be verified to Admiralty grade 1 (Confirmed) or 2 (Probably True) ONLY. Including unverified material in Confirmed Findings is FORBIDDEN.
   - MUST be cross-referenced to the Evidence Register entry and Admiralty grade.
   - MUST be specific and falsifiable: "Subject A holds a directorship in Company B, incorporated in Jurisdiction X on [date], as confirmed by Companies House filing [reference]" — vague characterisations are FORBIDDEN.

   **Corroborated Findings:** Findings supported by multiple sources that have not reached confirmed status. Each entry MUST state corroboration status (e.g., "Two independent sources, neither formally authenticated").

   **Unverified Leads:** Specific unresolved lines of enquiry. Each entry MUST state: what the lead is, what source generated it, recommended follow-up action. YOU MUST NOT present these as findings — they are tasking recommendations only.

   **Negative Results:** ALWAYS include every pursued line of enquiry that produced no substantive result. Mandatory per t4.2. Each entry MUST state: what was searched, under what parameters, and why the absence is significant or expected.

   **Scope Limitations:** YOU MUST precisely state what the investigation did NOT cover and why. Preventing practitioners from over-extending the memo's findings is REQUIRED.

   **Conclusions and Assessment:** Brief synthesis linking confirmed/corroborated findings to the original IRs. YOU MUST use ICD 203 probability language and reference the Analysis of Alternatives (Phase 5).

   **Source Index:** You are REQUIRED to include an exhaustive numbered list of all sources referenced, linked to Source Register entries. A cited source absent from the index is FORBIDDEN.

3. ALWAYS apply these quality standards:
   - No conclusions without traceable facts.
   - No vague characterisations.
   - ICD 203 compliance throughout.
