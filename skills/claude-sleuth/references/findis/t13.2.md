# t13.2 — Structural Separation of Facts, Assumptions, and Judgments

**Template:** `working/briefing.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST structure the briefing body under three mandatory, visually distinct sections. Blending the three sections is a FORBIDDEN failure mode:

   **Facts (Intelligence):**
   - ALWAYS use only verified data points graded 1 (Confirmed) or 2 (Probably True) on the Admiralty credibility axis.
   - You are REQUIRED to include an inline or footnoted reference to the Source Register entry and Admiralty grade for every fact.
   - It is CRITICAL that this section contains NO interpretation. "Entity A holds a directorship in Company B (Companies House, A1)" is a fact. "Entity A's directorship suggests they control the network" is a judgment — it MUST appear in the Judgments section only.

   **Assumptions:**
   - YOU MUST list every assumption identified during ACH setup (t12.1) with its rationale.
   - You are REQUIRED to include an impact statement for each assumption: if this assumption is wrong, does the conclusion change?
   - Assumptions MUST appear under their own header — embedding them within Facts or Judgments is FORBIDDEN.

   **Judgments:**
   - ALWAYS derive analytical conclusions directly from the ACH conclusion (t12.3).
   - All probabilistic claims MUST use ICD 203 language (t13.3). Non-standard hedging ("might," "could be," "good chance") is FORBIDDEN.
   - YOU MUST include the **Analysis of Alternatives**: explain why competing hypotheses were rejected. Omitting this is FORBIDDEN.
   - Judgments affected by unfilled intelligence gaps MUST be explicitly caveated.

2. It is CRITICAL that you apply the Separation Principle throughout:
   - A reviewer MUST be able to independently verify each fact, challenge each assumption, and evaluate whether judgments logically follow.
   - In legal proceedings, this structure proves what was established through evidence versus what was inferred by the analyst — a merged structure is a complete systematic failure.
