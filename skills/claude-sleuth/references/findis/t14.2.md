# t14.2 — Maintaining the Dissemination Log

**Template:** `database/task-log.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST record every act of sharing intelligence products at the moment of dissemination — retrospective logging is FORBIDDEN. You are REQUIRED to include the following in each entry:
   - Unique sequential Dissemination Reference Number.
   - UTC-normalised date and time of dissemination.
   - Recipient identity (individual and/or organisation; in law enforcement contexts: rank, role, agency).
   - Exact material shared, identified by case file reference number — a general description is FORBIDDEN.
   - Classification and handling restrictions on the disseminated material (including 5×5×5 handling code where applicable).
   - Authorising officer identity.
   - Purpose of dissemination (for action / for information / for legal proceedings / for supervisory review).

2. ALWAYS record all restrictions on further dissemination:
   - Whether the recipient is authorised to share the material further, and with whom.
   - Source protection restrictions: YOU MUST communicate restrictions preventing source identity disclosure to the recipient and record this. Failing to communicate source protection restrictions is FORBIDDEN.
   - Redaction requirements: if provided in redacted form, YOU MUST document the redactions and their reasons.

3. In investigations leading to legal proceedings, YOU MUST track disclosure obligations:
   - Material shared with prosecution teams MUST be recorded against CPIA disclosure rules; note whether material is "used" or "unused" in the prosecution file.
   - Defence disclosure MUST be logged separately with specific legal basis.
   - Sensitive material schedules (MG6 forms): YOU MUST record existence without revealing content.

4. It is CRITICAL that these integrity standards are maintained:
   - Entries are IMMUTABLE once recorded. YOU MUST make corrections by adding a new entry referencing the original — modifying existing entries is FORBIDDEN.
   - The completed log MUST be archived alongside the Evidence Register and Source Register as part of the permanent case file.
