# t15.4 — Legal Disclosure Preparation (UK MG5 Standard)

**Template:** `working/report.md`

## Procedure

### Governing Legal Framework

The MG5 (Police Report) is the standardised CPS submission governed by CPIA 1996 and its Code of Practice. Three duties are non-negotiable and YOU MUST discharge all three:
- **Duty to Pursue All Reasonable Lines of Enquiry:** The Task Log (t4.1) and Negative Results (t4.2) are the primary evidence this duty was discharged. Unpursued lines of enquiry MUST appear in the Policy File (t4.3) with documented rationale.
- **Duty to Record and Retain:** ALL material generated or obtained MUST be recorded and retained, regardless of whether it is used in prosecution. The entire Evidence Register is in scope — selective retention is FORBIDDEN.
- **Duty of Disclosure:** YOU MUST disclose to the defence any material that might reasonably undermine the prosecution case or assist the defence.

### MG5 Structure

1. **Case Summary Narrative:** YOU MUST provide a chronological factual account covering: nature of the alleged offence, how it came to investigators' attention, sequence of investigative actions, evidence obtained and how it was obtained, suspect's response.

2. **Points to Prove:** You are REQUIRED to explicitly state the legal elements required to establish the charged offence, with reference to the specific evidence addressing each element.

3. **Witness Summary:** ALWAYS list prosecution witnesses and their evidence, with statement references.

4. **Exhibit Schedule:** You are REQUIRED to list all physical and digital exhibits with unique reference numbers, descriptions, and storage locations. Every digital item MUST appear in the Evidence Register with a valid SHA-256 hash — an exhibit without a hash is FORBIDDEN.

5. **Factual Language:** It is CRITICAL that the MG5 is stripped of analytical language, intelligence assessments, probabilistic judgments, personal opinions, and investigative theory. The following are FORBIDDEN: "the suspect clearly intended to", "this strongly suggests", any attribution of motive or character. All investigative methodology not already known to the defence MUST be removed from the MG5.

### Sensitive Material Separation (MG6 Schedules)

6. **MG6C — Sensitive Material:** YOU MUST schedule material claimed as public interest immunity (covert source identities, surveillance techniques, ongoing operations). Record existence and basis for sensitivity without revealing content.

7. **MG6D — Retained Non-Sensitive Unused Material:** YOU MUST schedule material retained in the investigation file but not in the prosecution file and not claimed as sensitive.

8. It is CRITICAL that you understand: omission is NOT an acceptable alternative to protected scheduling. Material MUST be scheduled — either in the prosecution file or on MG6. Silent omission is FORBIDDEN and constitutes a prosecutorial failure.

### Quality Standards

9. ALWAYS ensure every sentence in the MG5 is a verifiable factual statement attributable to a specific source. Contested facts MUST record both versions with their respective sources.
10. YOU MUST conduct a Final CPIA Compliance Audit before submission: all Evidence Register material is scheduled, no sensitive material in the MG5, negative results (t4.2) are referenced in the narrative or unused material schedules. Submitting without a completed audit is FORBIDDEN.
11. Where the investigation involves complex financial structures, offshore jurisdictions, or frontier digital evidence: you are REQUIRED to conduct this in consultation with the CPS reviewing lawyer before submission.
