# t13.4 — Mandatory Source Summary Statement

**Template:** `working/briefing.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST place the Source Summary Statement immediately after the BLUF — before the detailed analysis. It MUST be read before the findings. Relegating it to an appendix is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to include the following content:

   **Source Base Overview:**
   - Number of independent sources.
   - Distribution across tiers (Tier 1 government records, Tier 2 commercial databases, Tier 3 social media, Tier 4 commentary).
   - Proportion of technical sources vs. human sources vs. open sources.
   - Aggregate Admiralty grade distribution across the evidence base.

   **Significant Gaps and Limitations:**
   - Missing source types (entire categories of evidence that were inaccessible).
   - Jurisdictional gaps (registries or jurisdictions not searchable).
   - Temporal gaps (source base covers only part of the relevant period).
   - Collection constraints (legal restrictions, resource limits, access barriers).

   **Single-Source Dependencies:**
   - YOU MUST list every analytical judgment that rests primarily on one source.
   - ALWAYS state the source's Admiralty grade and the judgment it supports.
   - You are REQUIRED to assess the risk: if this source is discredited, the dependent conclusion collapses.

3. It is CRITICAL that limitations are presented honestly. Downplaying gaps or inflating source quality is a FORBIDDEN failure mode — the integrity of the entire briefing depends on the transparency of this section.
