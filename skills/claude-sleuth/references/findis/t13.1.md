# t13.1 — Constructing the Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF)

**Template:** `working/briefing.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST place the BLUF as the very first paragraph of the analytical briefing — before any narrative, evidence section, or methodology discussion. It MUST be 3–5 sentences:
   - **Lead Sentence:** The primary analytical conclusion stated directly. Example: "Entity X is very likely (80–95%) the ultimate beneficial owner of the shell company network identified in this investigation."
   - **Key Supporting Points:** 1–3 sentences summarising the strongest differentiating evidence. No detailed elaboration — the reader MUST be able to understand the evidential basis at a glance.
   - **Critical Caveat:** If the conclusion rests on a fragile assumption, a single-source dependency, or a conclusion-critical intelligence gap, YOU MUST include this in the BLUF — burying it in the body is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to apply ICD 203 compliance throughout:
   - **Likelihood:** Use the 7-tier scale: "almost certainly" (95–99%), "very likely" (80–95%), "likely" (55–80%), "roughly even chance" (45–55%), "unlikely" (20–45%), "very unlikely" (5–20%), "remote" (1–5%).
   - **Confidence:** State separately as High, Moderate, or Low.
   - It is CRITICAL that confidence and likelihood NEVER appear in the same sentence — combining them is FORBIDDEN.

3. YOU MUST derive the BLUF directly from the ACH analysis (t12.3):
   - Lead sentence = the hypothesis with the lowest inconsistency score.
   - Key supporting points = the most diagnostic evidence items that differentiated the winner from its competitors.
   - Critical caveat = the Sensitivity Analysis (t12.3) and Deception/Gap Assessment (t12.4) findings.

4. ALWAYS apply these quality checks before finalising the BLUF:
   - The BLUF introduces NO new information not in the body of the report. Introducing new information in the BLUF is FORBIDDEN.
   - Language MUST be objective — emotive, persuasive, or advocacy phrasing is FORBIDDEN.
   - A decision-maker reading ONLY the BLUF must have an accurate understanding of the conclusion, its evidential basis, and its main limitations.
