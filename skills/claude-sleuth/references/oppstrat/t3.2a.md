# t3.2a — Defining Terms of Reference (ToR)

**Template:** `research/case-decision-log.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST draft Terms of Reference that explicitly state the legal or ethical mandate for the investigation. A ToR MUST be in place before any collection activity begins — proceeding without one is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to define the investigation's boundaries across four dimensions: temporal (date range), geographic (jurisdictions in scope), entity (subjects and entities in scope), and methodological (collection methods authorised).

3. It is CRITICAL that the ToR explicitly states what is OUT of scope. Scope creep is a systematic failure — any activity beyond the ToR MUST be logged as a Policy Deviation before it is undertaken.

4. ALWAYS cross-reference the ToR against the PLAN justification from t1.3. Any ToR element that cannot be justified under all four PLAN criteria MUST be removed or amended.

5. YOU MUST obtain supervisory sign-off on the ToR before collection begins. An unsigned ToR is FORBIDDEN as an operational document.
