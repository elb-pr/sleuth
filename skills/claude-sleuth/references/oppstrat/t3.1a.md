# t3.1a — Case Summary Record Initiation

**Template:** `research/case-decision-log.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST create the Case Summary Record at the outset of the investigation. Beginning collection without an open Case Summary Record is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to record the following fields at initiation: case reference number (unique, sequential), investigation title, lead investigator identity, date and time of case opening (UTC), and initial scope statement.

3. ALWAYS set the initial case status to "Active" and record the authorising officer who approved the investigation. An uninitialised or unauthorised case record is a complete systematic failure.

4. It is CRITICAL that the initial scope statement is precise — it defines the legal and operational boundaries of the investigation. Vague scope statements create audit vulnerabilities and must be rejected.

5. YOU MUST maintain the Case Summary Record as a living document. ALWAYS update it when scope changes, key milestones are reached, or the case status changes — treating it as a static document is FORBIDDEN.
