# t2.3 — Prioritising Lines of Enquiry

**Template:** `research/investigation-strategy.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST rank all lines of enquiry by diagnostic value — the degree to which pursuing this lead would change the analytical outcome. ALWAYS direct resources to the highest-diagnostic lines first.

2. You are REQUIRED to classify each line of enquiry against two axes: (a) diagnostic value and (b) collection feasibility. Lines with high diagnostic value and high feasibility are REQUIRED to be actioned first.

3. It is CRITICAL that low-diagnostic lines of enquiry are not allowed to consume resources ahead of high-diagnostic ones. Misallocation of investigative effort is a systematic failure.

4. ALWAYS document the ranking rationale in `research/investigation-strategy.md` — priority decisions made without recorded reasoning are FORBIDDEN.

5. YOU MUST review and update the priority ranking whenever a significant new finding changes the analytical picture. You are REQUIRED to log the updated ranking and the trigger event in `research/case-decision-log.md`.
