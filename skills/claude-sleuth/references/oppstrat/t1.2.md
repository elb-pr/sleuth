# t1.2 — Environmental Assessment (STEEPLES)

**Template:** `research/case-decision-log.md`

## Procedure

1. **Social:** YOU MUST map identity structures using `research/cultural-context.md`. You are REQUIRED to document formal and informal power hierarchies, patronage networks, and kinship-based control structures relevant to the subject.

2. **Technical:** Evaluate the subject's digital footprint — domain ownership, hosting infrastructure, and technology stack. It is CRITICAL that you identify encryption, VPN use, or privacy-enhancing technologies that constitute collection barriers. Map adversarial reconnaissance vectors using MITRE ATT&CK.

3. **Economic:** YOU MUST analyse the financial environment for industrial composition, circular ownership structures, unexplained wealth, and transactions lacking apparent business rationale.

4. **Environmental:** Establish geospatial coordinates and physical boundaries. Apply map regression to track landscape changes over time. You are REQUIRED to run `geolocation.py` for coordinate confirmation. Identify terrain or infrastructure features that constrain collection or movement.

5. **Political:** YOU MUST identify Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and assess jurisdictional geopolitical tensions. Verify alignment with FATF standards.

6. **Legal:** YOU MUST map all relevant jurisdictions where data resides. You are REQUIRED to document compliance obligations (GDPR, CPIA, UK MG5) and confirm the collection plan satisfies disclosure standards.

7. **Ethical:** ALWAYS apply proportionality assessment to every privacy intrusion. You are REQUIRED to document purpose limitation and data minimisation compliance. Record role-specific constraints.

8. **Safety:** It is CRITICAL that you assess OPSEC risks to the investigator and to sources. YOU MUST document VPN configuration, account separation, and source protection measures. Record all risk management strategies in `research/case-decision-log.md`.

## Transition Gate

It is FORBIDDEN to progress until all eight factors have been assessed. An empty factor MUST NOT be skipped — you are REQUIRED to record it as a confirmed gap.
