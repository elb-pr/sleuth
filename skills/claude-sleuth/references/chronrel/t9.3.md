# t9.3 — Temporal Conflict Resolution

**Template:** `analysis/chronological-matrix.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST identify and log all conflicts during chronology assembly (t9.1):
   - **Timestamp Conflicts:** Two or more sources report different times for the same event.
   - **Narrative Conflicts:** Sources agree on timing but disagree on what occurred.

2. You are REQUIRED to apply source reliability comparison as the primary resolution mechanism:
   - System-generated timestamps (bank logs, email server headers, CCTV time-codes) MUST take precedence over human recollection.
   - Primary sources (directly recorded the event) MUST take precedence over secondary sources (reporting on the event).
   - YOU MUST compare Admiralty grades (t3.1b) — the higher-reliability source's timestamp takes precedence. The rejected timestamp MUST be preserved with its source attribution.

3. It is CRITICAL that every conflict terminates in one of three documented outcomes — leaving a conflict unresolved and undocumented is FORBIDDEN:
   - **Resolved — Preferred:** One source adopted; rejected timestamp retained with explanation. Update Temporal Certainty to "Resolved conflict — [source preferred]."
   - **Resolved — Reconciled:** Apparent conflict explained by a non-contradictory interpretation (e.g., timezone error, different phases of the same event). YOU MUST document the reconciliation.
   - **Unresolved — Flagged:** Both timestamps retained. Event marked "Conflicting" in Temporal Certainty. YOU MUST generate a new IR to seek disambiguating evidence.

4. ALWAYS treat conflicts as potentially analytically significant:
   - **Deception Indicators:** Systematic conflicts between a subject's self-reported timeline and independently verified timestamps MUST be entered as evidence in the ACH Matrix.
   - **Source Reliability Reassessment:** Systematic inaccuracies from one source MUST trigger downgrading of its Admiralty A–F rating and cascade-reassessment of all records it contributed.
   - **Alibi Destruction:** A conflict between a claimed location and a technical record (mobile tower data, transaction log) is high-priority — YOU MUST escalate it immediately.

5. YOU MUST maintain a Conflict Register (sub-section of the chronological matrix or separate annex): list every conflict, sources involved, resolution outcome, and reasoning. It is CRITICAL that unresolved conflicts are surfaced in the Final Briefing (t13.x) — presenting a clean timeline when underlying temporal evidence is disputed is FORBIDDEN.
