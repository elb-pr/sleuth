# t9.2 — Identifying Temporal Gaps

**Template:** `analysis/chronological-matrix.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST conduct a structured sweep for each key entity after assembling the raw chronology (t9.1): identify periods with no recorded events. For each entity-specific gap, you are REQUIRED to record the period, the entity, and the type of evidence missing.

2. It is CRITICAL that you insert placeholder entries at every gap location in the chronology (e.g., "Subject activity unknown between 14 March and 28 March"). Leaving gaps blank is FORBIDDEN — blank gaps allow false continuity to propagate into analysis.

3. YOU MUST classify every gap as one of the following:
   - **Evidential Absence:** The source does not cover this period. Analytically neutral.
   - **Investigative Gap:** The period is within scope but evidence has not yet been found. Demands additional collection.
   - **Obstructive Gap:** Evidence that should exist has been destroyed, concealed, or is inaccessible. This is itself a finding with potential legal significance.

4. ALWAYS assign a significance rating to each gap: would filling it potentially change the ACH Matrix outcome (t11.x)? YOU MUST flag sudden disruptions to regular patterns regardless of whether evidence exists for that period — pattern breaks are analytically significant.

5. For every Investigative Gap with significant diagnostic value, YOU MUST generate a new IR in `research/investigation-strategy.md` specifying the gap period, entity, and type of evidence sought. You are REQUIRED to feed this into the ICP via the Phase 4 Quality Checkpoint.

6. ALWAYS prioritise gap collection by diagnostic value — gaps that could alter the analytical conclusion MUST receive the highest collection priority.
