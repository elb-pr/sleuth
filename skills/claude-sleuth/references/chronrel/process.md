# Chronological & Relational Processing

Phase 4 of the investigative workflow. Synthesis stage where structured entities are mapped to identify patterns, causality, and hidden networks.


### Step 9: Temporal Normalization & Chronological Analysis
The primary tool for this step is the **Chronological Matrix Template**, which creates a temporal backbone for the investigation. This process is essential for Establishing causality and resolving conflicting narratives.

*   **Task 9.1: Master Chronology Construction.** Analysts must aggregate all dated events from the evidence register into a single matrix. This involves normalizing all timestamps to a UTC standard to prevent sequencing errors in multi-jurisdictional or digital investigations.
*   **Task 9.2: Identifying Temporal Gaps.** The template requires investigators to explicitly document "unknowns" or placeholders where activity is unaccounted for. This gap analysis directs future resource collection and prevents the analyst from jumping to false conclusions.
*   **Task 9.3: Conflict Resolution.** When multiple sources provide conflicting timestamps for the same event, the analyst must use the matrix to compare source reliability and either resolve the conflict or flag the anomaly for further investigation.
*   **Task 9.4: Chronological Types.** Depending on the investigation's focus, analysts may develop sub-timelines, such as financial transaction sequences (tracing fund movements) or communication timelines (exposing relationship evolution).

### Step 10: Relational Processing & Network Visualization
Using the **Network Architecture (Edge List) Template**, investigators map the relationships between entities identified in Phase 3 to reveal the network's structure and vulnerabilities.

*   **Task 10.1: Edge List Generation.** Analysts compile structured tabular data containing four mandatory fields: Source, Target, Relationship Type, and Evidence Citation. Standard relationship types include "Owned By," "Communicated With," or "Director Of".
*   **Task 10.2: Quantitative Social Network Analysis (SNA).** Before visualization, the network is analyzed mathematically to identify:
    *   **Central Hubs:** Nodes with high "Degree Centrality" representing the most connected entities.
    *   **Bridge Nodes:** Nodes with high "Betweenness Centrality" that act as gatekeepers between isolated clusters.
*   **Task 10.3: Visual Grammar Application.** To preserve evidentiary integrity, the visualization must distinguish between "Confirmed" and "Unconfirmed" links. Solid lines represent proven associations, while dashed or dotted lines signify suspected or low-confidence relationships.
*   **Task 10.4: Two-Mode Analysis.** To identify hidden co-occurrences, analysts map people to shared events, locations, or corporations, revealing indirect affiliations that simple person-to-person graphs might obscure.

### Step 11: Specialized Spatial & Media Authentication
This step integrates specialized Analysis Templates to ground the investigation in physical reality and verify the authenticity of collected digital assets.

*   **Task 11.1: Content Verification.** Using the **Content Verification Report**, analysts authenticate photos or videos through a multi-step process: reverse image searching for original context, geolocation via visual cues, and chronolocation via shadow and weather analysis.
*   **Task 11.2: Map Regression & Urban Morphology.** The **`analysis/verification.md`** utilizes map regression—comparing sequences of historical maps—to track changes in landscape and urban form over time.
*   **Task 11.3: Spatial Econometrics.** The **`analysis/morphological.md`** analyzes how regional economies or specific sites have evolved, outputting significance assessments based on urban morphology and historical employment data.

### Phase 4 Quality & Integrity Checkpoints
To maintain professional rigor, the following standards are enforced:
*   **Traceability:** Every link on a chart and every entry on a timeline must be traceable back to a specific intelligence report or evidence artifact.
*   **Timezone Normalization:** All system logs and manual entries must be mathematically sound and normalized to UTC before analysis.
*   **Legend and Documentation:** Every visualization must include a legend defining node shapes, line styles, and the temporal scope of the data.
