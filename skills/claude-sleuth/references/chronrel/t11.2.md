# t11.2 — Map Regression and Urban Morphology

**Template:** `analysis/verification.md`

YOU MUST use the **`analysis/verification.md`** to analyse how specific locations have changed over time. By comparing sequences of historical maps, satellite imagery, and aerial photographs, the investigator tracks physical development, land use changes, and structural alterations that provide contextual intelligence about the investigation's geographic focus areas.

## Procedure

### 1. Map Regression Methodology
YOU MUST conduct map regression — the systematic comparison of cartographic representations of the same location across different time periods.
- **Source Acquisition:** You are REQUIRED to assemble a chronological series of maps and imagery covering the location of interest: historical Ordnance Survey maps (UK), USGS topographic maps (US), declassified aerial photography, commercial satellite imagery archives (Google Earth, Sentinel Hub), and institutional map collections.
- **Temporal Layering:** YOU MUST georeference maps from different periods so that changes can be identified through direct overlay comparison. Each map layer MUST be dated and sourced — undated layers are FORBIDDEN.
- **Change Identification:** You are REQUIRED to systematically document all changes between each period: new construction, demolition, land use conversion, road network changes, boundary alterations, and landscape modification.

### 2. Urban Morphology Analysis
YOU MUST conduct urban morphology analysis to examine how the physical form of the built environment has evolved:
- **Building Footprint Analysis:** It is CRITICAL that changes in building footprints between map periods are tracked — this is particularly valuable for investigations involving property development, planning fraud, or disputed land ownership.
- **Land Parcel History:** YOU MUST track how land parcels have been subdivided, merged, or rezoned — this provides intelligence about ownership changes and development intentions that may not be reflected in registry records alone.
- **Infrastructure Context:** You are REQUIRED to document the development of roads, utilities, and transport links around each location of interest.

### 3. Investigative Applications
YOU MUST apply map regression outputs to the following investigative functions:
- **Property Investigation:** Trace physical development to identify when structures were built, modified, or demolished — YOU MUST compare against planning applications, building permits, and insurance records.
- **Historical Context:** ALWAYS interpret archival evidence against the historical character of the location at the time — a photograph carries different analytical weight depending on whether the address was residential, commercial, or vacant.
- **Environmental and Planning Intelligence:** YOU MUST identify unauthorised construction, environmental damage, or planning violations through comparison of permitted development against observed physical changes.

### 4. Output: Place History Report
YOU MUST compile a structured **Place History Report**:
- **Timeline of Physical Change:** A chronological record of all documented changes, sourced from the map and imagery comparison.
- **Current State Assessment:** A description of the location's present condition, verified against the most recent available imagery.
- **Entity Register Integration:** You are REQUIRED to link the Place History Report to the relevant Location entities in the Entity Register — an unlinked report is a complete systematic failure.
