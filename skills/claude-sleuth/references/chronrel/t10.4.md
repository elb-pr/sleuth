# t10.4 — Two-Mode Analysis

**Tool:** `network_analyzer.py`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST select a Mode 2 (affiliation) category based on the investigation's analytical focus:
   - **Person-to-Event:** Co-attendance at meetings, conferences, or incidents — reveals hidden coordination.
   - **Person-to-Organisation:** Shared directorships, shareholdings, employment — primary method for detecting nominee director networks and beneficial ownership structures.
   - **Person-to-Location:** Shared addresses, jurisdictions, or travel destinations — indicates operational coordination.
   - **Person-to-Account:** Shared access to financial accounts, phone numbers, or digital resources — strong indicator of operational trust.

2. YOU MUST build the two-mode matrix from the Entity Register relationship links. You are REQUIRED to include only directly evidenced affiliations from the edge list (t10.1).

3. YOU MUST project to a one-mode network: for every pair of Person entities sharing a Mode 2 affiliation, create a direct implied edge weighted by the number of shared affiliations (multiplexity). Individuals sharing affiliations across multiple Mode 2 categories MUST receive higher edge weights.

4. You are REQUIRED to apply SNA metrics (t10.2) to the projected one-mode network and identify:
   - **Hubs:** Individuals with many shared affiliations.
   - **Bridges:** Individuals connecting otherwise separate clusters of co-affiliated entities.

5. It is CRITICAL that the projected network is clearly annotated as representing implied, NOT proven, relationships. Two-mode analysis generates hypotheses for investigation — presenting two-mode projections as conclusions is FORBIDDEN. YOU MUST annotate the visual output (t10.3) to clearly distinguish directly evidenced relationships from those inferred through two-mode projection.

6. ALWAYS document analytical findings: shell company networks exposed by shared director patterns, event-based coordination patterns suggesting avoidance of digital communication, geographic co-location in unusual jurisdictions.
