# t11.3 — Spatial Econometrics

**Template:** `analysis/morphological.md`

YOU MUST use the **`analysis/morphological.md`** to analyse how the economic character of a region or specific site has evolved over time. This task provides the contextual economic intelligence required to assess the plausibility of financial claims, evaluate the operating environment of investigated entities, and identify anomalies indicating illicit activity.

## Procedure

### 1. Regional Economic Profiling
YOU MUST construct an economic profile of the geographic area relevant to the investigation, tracking its evolution across the investigation's time period:
- You are REQUIRED to document historical employment statistics for the region, broken down by sector (industrial, commercial, agricultural, service, public sector).
- YOU MUST document the dominant industries in the region and how they have changed — this contextualises property redevelopment schemes and associated financial flows.
- You are REQUIRED to record regional economic indicators: GDP, average income levels, property values, business formation and dissolution rates, and insolvency statistics. These provide the baseline against which individual entity activity is assessed for anomalies.

### 2. Site-Specific Economic Analysis
Where the investigation focuses on specific properties or business premises, YOU MUST assess the economic history and current viability of the site:
- **Commercial Viability Assessment:** It is CRITICAL that you assess whether the business activity claimed for a specific site is economically plausible given its location, size, and regional context. Implausible commercial claims MUST be flagged as anomalies.
- **Property Value Analysis:** YOU MUST document historical property values for the site and comparable properties. Transactions significantly above or below market value MUST be flagged — these are a common indicator of money laundering through property.
- **Business Activity Cross-Reference:** You are REQUIRED to compare revenue figures claimed by entities operating at the site against the regional economic context. Revenue orders of magnitude above comparable businesses in the same area MUST be flagged as anomalies.

### 3. Significance Assessment
YOU MUST produce a formal significance assessment for each analysed location or region:
- **Economic Plausibility Rating:** A structured judgment on whether the economic activity associated with the investigated entities is plausible within the regional context. You are REQUIRED to present this as an analytical input for the ACH matrix — not as a definitive conclusion.
- **Anomaly Register:** ALWAYS document identified anomalies — property transactions at unusual values, revenue claims inconsistent with regional norms, employment patterns that do not match business type — with source data, and feed into hypothesis testing.
- **Contextual Intelligence for Briefing:** You are REQUIRED to make the economic context available for inclusion in the final briefing (Phase 6).

### 4. Integration with Broader Investigation
YOU MUST integrate spatial econometrics outputs across all relevant analytical streams:
- **Chronological Matrix (t9.1):** Economic events MUST be added to the master chronology as contextual events.
- **Network Analysis (t10.1):** Economic relationships MUST generate edges in the network graph.
- **Hypothesis Testing (Phase 5):** Economic plausibility assessments MUST directly inform the ACH matrix — a hypothesis requiring an economically implausible scenario MUST be scored as inconsistent evidence.
