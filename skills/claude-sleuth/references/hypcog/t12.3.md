# t12.3 — Quantitative Analysis and Drawing Conclusions

**Template:** `analysis/ach.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST calculate the Inconsistency Score for each hypothesis (each column):
   - **Simple count:** Total the I and II ratings. II may be double-weighted.
   - **Weighted count (preferred):** Multiply each inconsistency by the evidence item's reliability weight (derived from its Admiralty grade). An A1-graded II carries more weight than an F5-graded I.
   - You are REQUIRED to record both scores in a summary row at the bottom of the matrix.

2. YOU MUST identify the Most Likely Explanation: the hypothesis with the **lowest inconsistency score**. It is CRITICAL that you understand this is NOT the hypothesis with the most CC/C ratings — it is the one the evidence has the hardest time disproving.

3. ALWAYS assess and document the margin:
   - **Clear winner** (significantly lower score than all others): relatively confident conclusion.
   - **Close contest** (two or more hypotheses with similar scores): YOU MUST acknowledge ambiguity explicitly. The evidence base does not discriminate — additional collection is REQUIRED.

4. You are REQUIRED to document rejected hypotheses: for every rejected hypothesis, record the specific evidence items that drove the rejection. This documentation is REQUIRED for the Final Briefing and legal disclosure — undocumented rejections are FORBIDDEN.

5. YOU MUST run Sensitivity Analysis:
   - Identify which evidence items, if removed, would change the hypothesis ranking. These are the conclusion's critical dependencies.
   - YOU MUST remove all evidence graded F or 5/6 on the Admiralty scale and recalculate. If the winning hypothesis changes, the conclusion is fragile — it MUST be caveated accordingly.
   - It is CRITICAL that you identify which assumptions (t12.1) are most critical. Unverified assumptions on which the conclusion depends MUST be disclosed.
   - ALWAYS flag any single-source dependency.

6. YOU MUST state the conclusion with:
   - **ICD 203 confidence level** (High / Moderate / Low) — reflects evidence quality and reasoning soundness.
   - **ICD 203 probability expression** — "almost certainly" (95–99%), "very likely" (80–95%), "likely" (55–80%), "roughly even chance" (45–55%), "unlikely" (20–45%), "very unlikely" (5–20%), "remote" (1–5%).
   - It is FORBIDDEN to combine confidence and likelihood in the same sentence.

7. You are REQUIRED to produce three deliverables for Phase 6:
   - The scored matrix with inconsistency scores and pruning annotations.
   - The Sensitivity Analysis Summary.
   - The Analytical Conclusion with ICD 203 language.
