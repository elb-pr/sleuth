# t7.1 — Entity Resolution Pipeline (Fellegi-Sunter)

**Tool:** CSDb MCP

## Procedure

1. It is CRITICAL that you calibrate the pipeline to minimise **false positives** (erroneous merges of distinct entities) at the expense of tolerating more **false negatives** (missed links). In investigative contexts, a wrongful merge is more damaging than a missed connection.

2. YOU MUST run the resolution pipeline in four sequential stages — skipping any stage is FORBIDDEN:
   - **Stage 1 — Deterministic Pass:** Exact-match on unique identifiers (t6.3). Records matched here are committed with highest confidence.
   - **Stage 2 — Near-Deterministic Pass:** Match on combinations of high-reliability fields (full name + DOB + address). Normalise minor variations ("St." vs "Street", middle initial absent) before comparison.
   - **Stage 3 — Probabilistic Pass (Fellegi-Sunter):** Apply weighted field comparisons. Agreement on rare attribute values carries greater weight than agreement on common ones. Apply fuzzy string matching (Jaro-Winkler, Levenshtein, Soundex/Metaphone) to handle spelling variations, transliteration, and data entry errors.
   - **Stage 4 — Human Review:** Records scoring between the auto-link and non-match thresholds MUST be presented to an analyst for manual adjudication.

3. You are REQUIRED to define and document two thresholds: the score above which records are automatically linked, and the score below which records are declared non-matches. Threshold justification MUST be recorded — undocumented thresholds are FORBIDDEN for legal defensibility.

4. ALWAYS document every matching rule: which fields are compared, what normalisation is applied, what weights are assigned. A third party MUST be able to reproduce and audit every resolution decision — undocumented rules are a complete systematic failure.

5. YOU MUST preserve every version of matching rules if revised during the investigation, with associated rationale. Overwriting previous configurations is FORBIDDEN.

6. Recommended tooling: **Splink** (probabilistic record linkage at scale), **dedupe** (ML-based, active learning), **RapidFuzz** (fuzzy matching), **jellyfish** (phonetic name matching).
