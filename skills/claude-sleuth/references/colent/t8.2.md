# t8.2 — Specialised Research Templates

**Template:** `database/subject-profiles.md`, `research/cultural-context.md`

## Procedure

### Genealogical and Family Network Research

Invoke when the investigation involves kinship-based control structures, inheritance disputes, dynastic business ownership, or any context where family relationships are operationally significant.

1. YOU MUST apply the **Genealogical Proof Standard (GPS)**: reasonably exhaustive source search, complete citation of each source, analysis and correlation of collected information, resolution of conflicting evidence, and a soundly reasoned written conclusion. Skipping any GPS component is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to type and source every family relationship: parent-child, sibling, spouse, in-law, adoptive. Add to the Entity Register as `FAMILY_OF`, `HEIR_TO`, `SPOUSE_OF` links with full GPS compliance documentation.

3. It is CRITICAL that you seek both confirming AND disconfirming evidence for every claimed relationship. DNA results, birth/marriage certificates, parish records, census data, and immigration records are primary sources. Naming pattern analysis provides circumstantial supporting evidence only — it MUST NOT be treated as primary evidence.

4. YOU MUST map inheritance and succession patterns to identify how control of assets, companies, or land transfers across generations — this is CRITICAL for UBO identification where family trusts or customary tenure obscure formal ownership.

### Cultural Context and Ethnographic Background

Invoke when the investigation crosses cultural, ethnic, or geographic boundaries where the investigator lacks contextual understanding of local norms, power structures, or social organisation.

5. YOU MUST map formal and informal power structures: governmental hierarchies, clan/tribal leadership, religious authority, patronage networks, business oligarchies, diasporic community leadership. You are REQUIRED to record all findings in `research/cultural-context.md`.

6. ALWAYS document social norms affecting collection or interpretation: gender-segregated access, communal property ownership, language barriers, dialectal variation, culturally specific terminology. You are REQUIRED to record translation requirements and translator reliability.

7. It is CRITICAL that you conduct an explicit self-assessment of investigator assumptions about the community or culture. YOU MUST document and mitigate the risk of projecting familiar cultural frameworks onto unfamiliar contexts — undocumented assumption risk is a systematic failure.

8. ALWAYS record safety risks: risks to the investigator, to sources, and to subjects if the inquiry becomes known.
