# t8.1 — Subject and Corporate Profiles

**Template:** `database/subject-profiles.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST compile a **Subject Profile** for every natural person who is a target, subject of interest, or key associate:
   - **Identity:** Full name, aliases, DOB, nationality, physical description, ID document references — all sourced.
   - **Employment/Professional History:** Chronological record of employment, directorships, memberships. YOU MUST flag gaps as knowledge gaps for further collection.
   - **Digital Footprint:** Social media accounts (platform-specific IDs, not display names), email addresses, domain registrations, attributed online activity with attribution evidence cited.
   - **Financial Summary:** Bank accounts, property holdings, vehicles, shareholdings. You are REQUIRED to identify assets relevant to asset recovery, sanctions compliance, or money laundering.
   - **Relational Summary:** Known associates, family, and professional contacts from Entity Register relationship links — strength and source of each association MUST be noted.
   - **Risk Indicators:** PEP status, sanctions matches, adverse media, criminal record checks — each MUST include source and date of check.

2. YOU MUST compile a **Corporate Profile** for every legal entity in scope:
   - **Corporate Identity:** Legal name, trading names, registration number, jurisdiction, registered address, incorporation date. For dissolved entities, YOU MUST record dissolution date and circumstances.
   - **Ownership Chain:** YOU MUST map from the entity upward to the Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO). Record each intermediate holding entity, percentage ownership, and jurisdiction. Confidence level of UBO attribution MUST be stated.
   - **Circular Ownership Detection:** It is CRITICAL that you explicitly check for and document circular structures (A owns B owns C owns A) — these are red flags for money laundering, tax evasion, or sanctions circumvention.
   - **Directors and Officers:** Current and historical, cross-referenced against Subject Profiles.
   - **Financial Risk Assessment:** Revenue history, filing compliance, audit opinions, sanctions exposure. You are REQUIRED to compute the Altman Z-Score for insolvency risk where applicable.

3. ALWAYS include a **Knowledge Gaps** section in every profile listing what is missing and which IRs would need to be fulfilled to complete it. YOU MUST feed unresolved gaps back into the ICP (t2.2) — a profile with no knowledge gaps section is incomplete.

4. You are REQUIRED to state the profile's confidence level explicitly, derived from the aggregate Admiralty grades of the underlying source material.
