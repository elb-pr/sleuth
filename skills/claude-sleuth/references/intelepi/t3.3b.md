# t3.3b — OSINT Technical Verification

**Tool:** `content_archiver.py`, `geolocation.py`

## Procedure

1. **Reverse Image/Video Search:** YOU MUST submit image or video keyframes to reverse search engines (Google Reverse Image, TinEye, Yandex Images). If the media appears in an earlier, unrelated context, you are REQUIRED to flag it as potentially misleading and record the original context in the Source Register. A negative result is itself a supporting data point — ALWAYS document it.

2. **Geolocation:** YOU MUST identify distinctive visual cues in the media (street signs, architecture, vegetation, vehicle plates, commercial signage) and cross-reference against satellite imagery (Google Earth, Sentinel Hub) and street-level photography (Google Street View, Mapillary). You are REQUIRED to record matched latitude/longitude coordinates and confidence level. If the media cannot be geolocated, it MUST be recorded as a knowledge gap in the ICP.

3. **Chronolocation:** YOU MUST determine when the media was captured, independent of any metadata claims:
   - **Shadow analysis:** Use SunCalc with geolocated coordinates to calculate approximate capture time and date from shadow direction and length.
   - **Weather cross-reference:** Compare visible conditions against historical records (Open-Meteo, Weather Underground) for the geolocated area.
   - **Vegetation/seasonal analysis:** Use leaf coverage, crop state, or flowering patterns to narrow the temporal window.
   - **Temporal markers:** Newspaper front pages, event posters, construction progress — ALWAYS record all hard chronological anchors.

4. **Metadata Extraction:** YOU MUST extract EXIF data using ExifTool or PyExifTool and compare GPS coordinates and timestamps against geolocation and chronolocation findings. Metadata absent due to platform stripping is EXPECTED and is NOT an indicator of tampering. Metadata that is present but inconsistent with visual evidence is a significant red flag and MUST be escalated.

5. It is CRITICAL that you classify the media as: **Verified**, **Partially Verified**, **Unverified**, or **Falsified**. You are REQUIRED to update the Admiralty credibility grade (t3.1b) accordingly and link the Content Verification Report to the Evidence Register entry (t5.1) with its SHA-256 hash — an unclassified media item entering the analytical chain is FORBIDDEN.
