# t3.1b — Admiralty 6×6 Source Grading

**Tool:** `source_grader.py`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST assign an independent **Source Reliability** grade (A–F) for every discrete piece of information entering the investigation, based solely on the source's historical track record — NOT the plausibility of the current claim:
   - **A** — Completely Reliable
   - **B** — Usually Reliable
   - **C** — Fairly Reliable
   - **D** — Not Usually Reliable
   - **E** — Unreliable
   - **F** — Reliability Cannot Be Judged (default for all new/untested OSINT sources)

2. You are REQUIRED to assign an independent **Information Credibility** grade (1–6) based solely on the data's internal logic and external corroboration — NOT the source's track record:
   - **1** — Confirmed by independent sources
   - **2** — Probably True — unconfirmed but logically consistent
   - **3** — Possibly True — partially consistent
   - **4** — Doubtful
   - **5** — Improbable — actively contradicted
   - **6** — Truth Cannot Be Judged

3. It is CRITICAL that the two axes are assessed independently. Two cognitive errors are FORBIDDEN:
   - **Halo Effect:** Rating a source as A because the information seems plausible.
   - **Authority Bias:** Rating information as 1 because the source is historically reliable.

4. For anonymous digital sources defaulting to F: YOU MUST apply technical verification (t3.3b — geolocation, chronolocation, metadata). A technically confirmed "F1" rating is a legitimate and analytically valid outcome.

5. In UK law enforcement contexts, YOU MUST apply the **5×5×5 system**: append a handling code (1–5) specifying who may receive the information and what they may do with it.

6. ALWAYS record the combined alphanumeric grade (e.g., B2, F1) as mandatory metadata on every intelligence record in the Entity Register and Source Register. This grade MUST propagate through the entire analytical chain, including the ACH Sensitivity Analysis (t11.x) and the Final Briefing Source Summary (t13.4) — an ungraded record entering the analytical chain is a complete systematic failure.
