# t4.3 — Real-Time Policy Deviation Logging

**Template:** `research/case-decision-log.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST log a Policy Deviation whenever any investigative action falls outside the boundaries of the ICP, the ToR, or the PLAN framework. Categories include:
   - **Scope Expansion:** Investigating an entity, jurisdiction, or enquiry not in the original ToR.
   - **Method Change:** Using a collection method not authorised in the ICP.
   - **Priority Reordering:** Abandoning a planned line of enquiry in favour of an unplanned lead.
   - **Resource Reallocation:** Diverting time, personnel, or tools from planned to unplanned tasks.

2. It is CRITICAL that the deviation entry is logged at the moment the decision is made — BEFORE the new activity is undertaken. Post-hoc rationalisation is FORBIDDEN.

3. You are REQUIRED to use the standard decision matrix for every deviation entry:
   - **Situation:** What new information triggered the deviation?
   - **Aims:** What does the deviation seek to achieve?
   - **Influencing Factors (STEEPLES):** Any new Social, Technical, Economic, Environmental, Political, Legal, Ethical, or Safety factors?
   - **Assessment of Choices (PLAN):** Is the deviation Proportionate, Legal, Accountable, and Necessary?
   - **Risk Management:** What risks does the deviation introduce and how are they mitigated?

4. Authorisation MUST scale with risk:
   - **Minor** (sequence change): investigator logs and notes at next supervisory checkpoint.
   - **Significant** (new jurisdiction, entity, or method): supervisor approval is REQUIRED before proceeding.
   - **Fundamental** (ToR or legal basis change): formal re-authorisation — potentially involving legal counsel — is REQUIRED.

5. YOU MUST update `research/investigation-strategy.md` following any approved deviation to reflect the new scope. Reference the CDL deviation entry by its unique identifier and log any new actions in `templates/database/task-log.md` with a reference to the authorising CDL entry.
