# t4.2 — Mandatory Recording of Negative Results

**Template:** `templates/database/task-log.md`

## Procedure

1. ALWAYS record every null enquiry in `templates/database/task-log.md` with the same mandatory fields as a positive finding. Omitting a negative result is FORBIDDEN.

2. You are REQUIRED to include the following in every negative result entry:
   - Action reference number
   - Exact source queried (database name, registry, platform, or individual)
   - Precise search parameters used (query terms, date ranges, jurisdictions, identifiers) — sufficient for a third party to reproduce the search
   - UTC-normalised timestamp of the search
   - Explicit statement that the search produced no relevant records
   - Analyst assessment: is this absence analytically significant (inconsistent with known facts) or expected (source does not cover the relevant period)?

3. It is CRITICAL that you understand: negative results are legally mandated disclosable material under UK CPIA. Failure to record them is a prosecutorial failure — not an administrative omission.

4. An investigation with exclusively positive findings and no documented negative results is a red flag for confirmation bias. YOU MUST flag this pattern to the supervisor — the supervisor is REQUIRED to verify a plausible ratio of positive to negative results during the Phase 2 Checkpoint.

5. ALWAYS apply negative results in the ACH Matrix (t11.x): the absence of expected evidence is scored as "Inconsistent" with the hypothesis that predicts it. Negative results are not analytically neutral — they are evidence.

6. "File drawer" suppression of negative results — relegating them to informal notes, verbal briefings, or omitting them entirely — is FORBIDDEN and constitutes a complete systematic failure.
