# t3.2b — Parallel Sourcing and Corroboration

**Template:** `research/investigation-strategy.md`

## Procedure

1. YOU MUST identify sources across multiple independent tiers before treating any significant claim as verified intelligence:
   - **Tier 1:** Official government records (Companies House, land registry, court filings, electoral register).
   - **Tier 2:** Verified commercial databases (Orbis, Experian, sanctions lists, shipping databases).
   - **Tier 3:** Authenticated social media and self-published content.
   - **Tier 4:** Secondary news commentary and aggregated databases.

2. It is CRITICAL that you apply the Independence Requirement: two sources only provide genuine corroboration if they derive their information independently. If Source B learned the fact from Source A, citing both is circular reporting — it counts as one source and MUST be flagged as such.

3. For critical claims — those that will materially affect the analytical conclusion or form the basis of a legal finding — you are REQUIRED to seek a minimum of three independent sources before grading the information as "1 (Confirmed)" on the Admiralty credibility axis.

4. ALWAYS prioritise cross-tier corroboration: a claim supported by Tier 1 + Tier 3 + Tier 4 from independent origins is more robust than three Tier 4 sources. YOU MUST document the tier composition of every corroboration chain.

5. For every IR where a tier is unavailable, YOU MUST document this as a negative finding in `templates/database/task-log.md` — source exhaustion MUST be recorded. Silently skipping an unavailable source tier is FORBIDDEN.

6. For high-stakes claims, YOU MUST construct an information lineage diagram tracing each claim back to its primary source. Record in the Source Register (t5.3). Any apparent corroboration traced to a single origin MUST be downgraded and the circular dependency flagged — failure to flag circular reporting is a complete systematic failure.
